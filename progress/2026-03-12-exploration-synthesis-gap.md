## What was done

Created `Research/backlog/2026-03-12-exploration-synthesis-gap.md`.

### Research question captured

During periods of rapid exploration (such as AI/LLM adoption), individuals and teams duplicate
effort rather than building on what colleagues have already built. The item asks: what are the
cognitive, genetic, incentive-level, and ego-driven mechanisms behind this? And given that
exploratory work is now frequently done by AI agents (the human may not be able to explain
what was built), is human-to-human synthesis still the right mechanism, or should synthesis
be delegated to agents?

### Scope decisions

- Included evolutionary/genetic basis (Self-Determination Theory (SDT), DRD4 novelty-seeking,
  exploration–exploitation trade-off)
- Included incentive system failures (NIH syndrome, credit attribution, visibility asymmetry)
- Included ego/identity mechanisms (Not Invented Here (NIH), competence signalling)
- Included the agent-mediated knowledge gap (tacit knowledge, the knowing-explaining gap)
- Included agent-as-synthesiser as a viable intervention to evaluate
- Excluded general knowledge management system design and tool-level engineering (those are
  downstream backlog items if the research motivates them)

### Priority

Set to `high` — the problem is actively occurring now in organisations and the agent-synthesis
angle is novel and time-sensitive.

### Sources seeded
