### Key determination on time-to-value claim

The claim "The SaaS option delivers faster time-to-value (6–12 months versus 12–24 months for bespoke initial deployment)" is **not supported by independent evidence** for the complex FSC + nCino + legacy core integration use case that is the paper's primary focus. Independent review platforms show nCino implementations consistently taking 12–24 months to full operations. The 6–12 month figure may apply to simpler or partial deployments. This is the weakest element in the paper and should be explicitly qualified.

Sources that appear to support faster time-to-value are predominantly vendor-cited (Personetics, Bottomline, Swan) and apply to simpler SaaS deployment scenarios, not the complex banking integrations studied in the paper.

## Mini-Retro

1. **Did the process work?** Yes — web search via Tavily returned good independent sources (IBM, McKinsey, G2, Gartner, Capterra, ThoughtWorks) across all four research tasks. The distinction between vendor-cited and independent sources was maintained throughout.

2. **What slowed down or went wrong?** Social media first-hand accounts (Reddit, LinkedIn) are harder to access systematically — Tavily returns aggregated summaries rather than direct post links. The resulting evidence on social media skews toward meta-commentary rather than primary accounts.

3. **What single change would prevent this next time?** For social media research tasks, explicitly include Reddit/LinkedIn URL patterns in the search queries to surface direct threads rather than secondary summaries.

4. **Is this a pattern?** The difficulty accessing social media first-hand accounts is a recurring constraint across research tasks requiring practitioner testimonials. This should be noted as a known limitation in research items with this scope.
