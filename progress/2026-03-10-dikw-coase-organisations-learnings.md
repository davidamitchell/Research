**`Research/backlog/2026-03-10-nature-of-the-firm-coase-organisations.md`** (priority: high)
> The Nature of the Firm: why organisations exist, their fitness functions, and invariants

Research question: Why do organisations and business units exist when markets are efficient? What are the fitness functions and invariants that determine when organisational form is the correct coordination mechanism?

Core sources seeded:
- Coase, R.H. (1937). "The Nature of the Firm." ★★★★★
- Williamson, O.E. (1981). Transaction Cost Economics
- North, D.C. (1990). Institutions, Institutional Change and Economic Performance
- Skelton & Pais (2019). Team Topologies — interaction modes as Coase's governance modes
- Ford, Parsons & Kua (2017). Building Evolutionary Architectures — fitness functions

Key synthesis from problem statement preserved in Context section verbatim:
- Firms exist when internal coordination is cheaper than market contracting
- Team Topologies interaction modes = Coase's governance modes for software orgs
- North's informal institutions (culture, norms) are the primary transaction cost reducers
