# ISO/IEC 42001:2023 controls, adoption, reputation, and evolution

## Research Question

What is International Organization for Standardization (ISO) and International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC) 42001:2023 for an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS), which specific controls in Annex A and the main body mitigate key Artificial Intelligence (AI) risks, how widely it has been adopted and certified since publication, how it is perceived by enterprises, regulators, certification bodies, and AI ethics experts, and what development milestones, amendments, and planned revisions define its evolution?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- ISO/IEC 42001:2023 purpose, structure, clauses, and Annex A control catalogue.
- Mapping controls to core AI risk classes (bias, transparency, data quality, security, privacy, accountability, and ethical harms).
- Public evidence of adoption and certification activity by region, sector, and organisation type.
- Reputation signals from industry guidance, regulators, accredited certification bodies, and AI governance specialists.
- Development timeline, related standards (for example ISO/IEC 42002 and ISO/IEC 42006), and announced updates.

**Out of scope:**
- Certifying or auditing any specific organisation implementation.
- Providing legal advice or jurisdiction-specific compliance determinations.
- Reproducing paywalled standard text beyond publicly available summaries.

**Constraints:** (time, source types, access)
- Use publicly accessible sources and clearly separate primary sources from secondary commentary.
- Prefer the most recent post-2023 evidence for adoption and revisions.
- Deliver as a knowledge-oriented synthesis suitable for decision support.

## Context

This question informs how to evaluate ISO/IEC 42001 as a practical AI governance baseline for organisations deciding whether to adopt a certifiable management-system approach versus alternative frameworks.

## Approach

1. Establish what ISO/IEC 42001:2023 contains: scope, clause architecture, and Annex A control domains.
   1a. Identify control statements and implementation guidance that are publicly documented.
   1b. Classify controls by risk type and governance objective.
2. Map control families to major AI risks and assess practical implementation effectiveness and limitations.
   2a. Identify intended risk mitigations from normative clauses and guidance references.
   2b. Compare reported implementation challenges and common failure points.
3. Quantify and characterise adoption since publication.
   3a. Gather certification and adoption indicators from certification bodies, registries, and market reports.
   3b. Segment evidence by geography, sector, and organisation size where data allows.
4. Assess reputation and stakeholder perception.
   4a. Compare enterprise, regulator, certification, and ethics-expert perspectives.
   4b. Identify drivers, barriers, and notable implementation success stories.
5. Reconstruct standard history and evolution.
   5a. Build a timeline from drafting to publication and subsequent updates.
   5b. Document links to companion standards and planned revisions.
