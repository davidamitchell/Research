# What metrics beyond code acceptance rates best capture net organisational value when AI coding tools are adopted with productivity mandates, and how do speed-focused incentives create hidden quality costs in high-volume agentic workflows?

## Research Question

What metrics beyond code acceptance rates and lines of code best capture net organisational value when Artificial Intelligence (AI) coding tools such as GitHub Copilot are adopted with productivity mandates, and to what extent do speed-focused incentive structures create hidden quality costs—technical debt, error propagation, and systemic "review rubber-stamping"—in high-volume agentic AI workflows?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Empirical measurement frameworks for AI coding tool productivity that go beyond acceptance rates and velocity metrics
- Evidence on how speed-focused Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) and Objectives and Key Results (OKRs) drive quality degradation, technical debt accumulation, and rubber-stamp code review behaviours
- Balanced scorecard approaches that combine speed, quality, oversight effectiveness, and long-term capability metrics for AI tool adoption
- Organisational reward structures—bonuses, performance reviews, team metrics—and their specific interaction with AI coding tool adoption mandates
- Governance interventions that demonstrably align individual productivity incentives with enterprise quality and risk outcomes

**Out of scope:**
- Machine learning (ML) model quality metrics unrelated to organisational incentive structures
- Consumer-tier AI product measurement outside enterprise contexts
- General software engineering metrics not specific to AI-augmented development
- Full compensation system design or Human Resources (HR) policy per jurisdiction

**Constraints:**
- Ground all claims in observable enterprise patterns and flag inferences clearly
- Expand all acronyms on first use
- Distinguish empirical evidence from theoretical frameworks
- Prior completed research on incentive misalignment and Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) rubber-stamping provides the foundational context; this item must extend rather than duplicate it

## Context

Existing repository research has established that governance circumvention is frequently driven by organisational incentives, and that HITL review degrades under volume pressure. [fact; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-04-26-ai-governance-culture-incentives-behaviour.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-02-hitl-review-volume-bottleneck-rubber-stamp.html]

The remaining gap is empirical evidence on the specific metrics that expose the gap between visible speed gains and invisible quality costs when productivity mandates drive AI tool adoption—particularly what a balanced measurement system should include and which governance levers demonstrably close the gap. [inference; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-02-incentive-misalignment-shadow-ai-skill-decay-controls.html]

## Approach

1. **Sub-question 1 — Measurement gap:** What metrics (beyond acceptance rate, velocity, lines of code) have been proposed or validated empirically to capture net value versus hidden costs (technical debt, error rates, review quality degradation) in AI-augmented development?

2. **Sub-question 2 — Incentive–quality interaction:** What is the empirical evidence that local speed incentives cause systemic quality degradation—rubber-stamping, accumulated debt, error propagation—in high-volume agentic AI systems? Are there natural experiments or longitudinal studies?

3. **Sub-question 3 — Balanced scorecard and governance interventions:** What balanced scorecard models (speed + quality + oversight effectiveness + long-term capability) have been proposed or tested? Which governance interventions—governed "fast lanes," mandatory quality gates, dual-metric OKRs—demonstrably realign individual and enterprise outcomes?
