# How Do Formal Governance Structures Distort Cross-Department Knowledge Flows?

## Research Question

How do formal governance mechanisms such as hierarchy, reporting lines, and mandatory protocols reshape the knowledge-flow landscape, and when do they unintentionally raise cross-department transaction costs?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Effects of formal governance structures on cross-boundary knowledge transfer.
- Tensions between compliance, control, and adaptive collaboration.
- Conditions under which governance enables versus suppresses useful lateral exchange.

**Out of scope:**
- Legal analysis of national labour law regimes.
- Detailed redesign of one specific company structure.

**Constraints:** Prioritise institutional and organisational-design evidence that links formal rules to measurable collaboration outcomes.

## Context

Formal structure is necessary for accountability, but can impose hidden friction on collaboration. Organisations need a way to distinguish productive governance from governance that unintentionally blocks knowledge flow.

## Approach

1. Identify formal governance features that correlate with increased transfer friction.
2. Analyse interaction effects between formal controls and informal coordination norms.
3. Produce design criteria for governance that preserves accountability without knowledge-flow lock-in.

## Sources

- [ ] [Adler, P. S. & Borys, B. (1996) Two Types of Bureaucracy: Enabling and Coercive](https://doi.org/10.2307/2391486) — framework for governance structures that help versus hinder work.
- [ ] [Ostrom, E. (1990) Governing the Commons](https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511807763) — institutional-design principles for rules that support collective action.
