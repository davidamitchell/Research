# IT Throughput Constraint Magnitude and Debt Accumulation Rate

## Research Question

What is the empirical relationship between Information Technology (IT) throughput capacity and systems capability debt accumulation rate across comparable organisations, and what proportion of do-mode automation demand is closeable by build-mode software delivery within three years under realistic AI-assisted productivity assumptions?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Evidence on debt accumulation versus software delivery throughput in large enterprises.
- Classification of automation demand into software-closeable versus structurally resistant categories.
- Sensitivity analysis over defensible AI-assisted development productivity ranges.

**Out of scope:**
- Building an enterprise-specific forecasting model.
- Sector-independent universal productivity assumptions without confidence bounds.

**Constraints:**
- Use comparable organisation size and sector cohorts where available.
- Keep assumptions about AI productivity multipliers explicit and sensitivity-tested.

## Context

The thesis treats IT throughput as the binding constraint that generates workaround demand. This item quantifies whether the bottleneck magnitude supports build-mode-first investment under realistic delivery assumptions.

## Approach

1. Gather throughput and backlog-growth evidence to estimate debt accumulation regimes.
2. Analyse low-code and RPA use-case catalogues for software-closeable versus structurally resistant demand types.
3. Run sensitivity analysis on do/build prioritisation under multiple AI-assisted productivity assumptions.
