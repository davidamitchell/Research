# Do-Mode Demand Persistence and Build-Mode Displacement

## Research Question

What evidence exists that do-mode Artificial Intelligence (AI) and automation investment displaces build-mode software delivery investment, and what is the observed persistence rate of do-mode workarounds after the underlying systems capability gap has been closed?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Comparative evidence from low-code, Robotic Process Automation (RPA), and AI-agent programmes.
- Decommission outcomes after software delivery closes the original capability gap.
- Governance and incentive mechanisms that reduce displacement of build-mode investment.

**Out of scope:**
- Prescriptive target operating model design for a specific enterprise.
- Evaluation of non-enterprise consumer automation tools.

**Constraints:**
- Use comparable organisations and time windows where possible.
- Distinguish correlation from causal displacement claims.

## Context

The thesis requires empirical support for two behavioural mechanisms: resource crowd-out from build mode to do mode, and workaround persistence after the root gap is closed. This item tests both mechanisms directly.

## Approach

1. Review empirical studies and benchmark datasets for low-code, RPA, and agent investment outcomes.
2. Extract persistence and decommission rates for automation workarounds after corresponding software delivery events.
3. Evaluate governance and incentive designs that successfully protect build-mode capacity.
