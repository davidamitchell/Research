# Basel, International Organization for Standardization (ISO), and National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST): classifying shadow workforce-system risk

## Research Question

How do Basel Committee on Banking Supervision (BCBS), ISO 31000, and NIST frameworks classify risk when business-critical workforce data is maintained in unmanaged tools instead of a formal system of record?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Basel Committee on Banking Supervision (BCBS) operational-risk framing
- ISO 31000 risk-principles mapping
- NIST risk and control taxonomy alignment

**Out of scope:**
- Product procurement recommendations
- Internal remediation design for a specific enterprise

**Constraints:** Use primary framework publications and provide side-by-side taxonomy mapping.

## Context

Risk, audit, and workforce teams need a shared classification model before controls or remediation can be prioritised consistently.

## Approach

1. Extract relevant definitions and categories from Basel, ISO, and NIST primary texts.
2. Compare similarities and differences in how each framework classifies this risk pattern.
3. Produce a normalized cross-framework classification table.
