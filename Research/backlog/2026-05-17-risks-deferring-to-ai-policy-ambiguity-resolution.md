# Risks of deferring to Artificial Intelligence (AI) for policy and ambiguity resolution

## Research Question

How does organisational reliance on Large Language Models (LLMs) for interpreting ambiguous policy and compliance requirements change risk outcomes across epistemic certainty, accountability, hallucination exposure, institutional learning, behaviour, adversarial misuse, and long-term policy governance?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Human decision quality effects when employees use LLM outputs to resolve policy ambiguity
- Accountability, escalation behaviour, and auditability impacts of AI-assisted policy interpretation
- Hallucination, relevance mismatch, and context-synthesis failure modes in mixed public and proprietary policy contexts
- Behavioural dynamics including confirmation bias, cognitive closure, and prompt iteration for desired outcomes
- Adversarial prompting and "interpretation laundering" risks in compliance environments
- Organisational drift risks (parallel policy frameworks, policy decay, and risk-profile shift)

**Out of scope:**
- Building or evaluating a specific vendor tool implementation
- Jurisdiction-specific legal advice for any single company
- Full forensic analysis of one organisation's private policy corpus

**Constraints:** Use public evidence from governance standards, behavioural science, audit/compliance guidance, and peer-reviewed research; distinguish demonstrated findings from hypotheses where direct enterprise data is unavailable.

## Context

The issue describes multiple independent but related risk classes created when staff defer policy interpretation to AI; this research should produce a decision-ready framework for when AI assistance is acceptable, when human escalation is mandatory, and which controls reduce governance failure.

## Approach

1. Quantify epistemic-risk effects.
   1a. How does hedging absence in LLM language affect user confidence calibration and compliance risk perception?
   1b. How strongly do fluent formatting and syntax correlate with bypassing formal verification channels?
   1c. What is the gap between model self-reported confidence and measured interpretation accuracy on ambiguous bylaws?
2. Analyse accountability and governance displacement.
   2a. How does AI-assisted interpretation reallocate legal and organisational liability?
   2b. Does AI second-opinion availability reduce escalation to risk and compliance teams?
   2c. How are AI-mediated decisions justified during audits versus peer consultation decisions?
3. Characterise context-synthesis and hallucination failure modes.
   3a. What failures arise when generic legal knowledge is mixed with proprietary internal policy?
   3b. How do training-data priors skew interpretations of local risk appetite?
   3c. How often do users miss relevance hallucinations that sound plausible but are inapplicable?
4. Evaluate institutional-knowledge and social-friction effects.
   4a. How does AI-first clarification change institutional memory diffusion across teams?
   4b. Does reduced social friction degrade baseline policy expertise over time?
   4c. What are long-run effects on mentoring and collaborative problem-solving?
5. Assess behavioural acceptance dynamics.
   5a. How does cognitive-closure pressure increase acceptance of flawed AI answers?
   5b. Which interventions reliably force deliberation before acting on AI-confirming outputs?
   5c. Does repeated prompt iteration increase confirmation-bias expression in policy decisions?
6. Measure authority drift and policy decay.
   6a. How quickly can unverified AI interpretations become de facto operational policy?
   6b. How do unofficial AI-derived interpretations diverge from executive and board intent?
7. Test adversarial policy-exploitation risk.
   7a. How vulnerable are compliance assistants to interpretation laundering prompts?
   7b. Which controls can detect deliberate prompt manipulation for high-risk shortcuts?
8. Analyse systemic feedback-loop risks.
   8a. What degradation rate appears when new policy drafts are derived from prior AI interpretations?
   8b. Could common model dependence create cross-industry compliance blind spots?
