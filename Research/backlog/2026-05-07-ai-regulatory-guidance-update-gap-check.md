# Artificial Intelligence regulatory guidance delta check: new advice, policy, and missed coverage since prior global financial-services review

## Research Question

Since the completion of `2026-04-24-ai-agent-regulation-global-financial-services`, what newly issued regulatory advice, policy, guidance, or supervisory statements have been published on Artificial Intelligence (AI) use in financial services across key jurisdictions, and what material coverage gaps (if any) were missed in the prior research item?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Delta scan since 2026-04-24 across the same jurisdictions used in the earlier item: European Union (EU), New Zealand (NZ), Australia, United Kingdom (UK), United States (US), and Canada
- Newly issued or materially updated regulator outputs: guidance notes, supervisory statements, policy papers, consultation updates, implementation guidance, enforcement signals, and timeline updates
- Gap analysis against the earlier item: what was omitted, underweighted, or no longer current
- Distinction between binding requirements, non-binding supervisory expectations, and advisory best-practice material

**Out of scope:**
- Repeating unchanged background material already captured in the prior item
- Non-financial-services sector guidance unless a regulator explicitly issues cross-sector rules that directly alter financial-services obligations
- Vendor marketing commentary without primary regulatory evidence

**Constraints:**
- Prioritise primary regulator or legislative sources first, then high-quality secondary sources only where needed for chronology or interpretation
- Every source must include a URL
- Treat this as an update and quality-review pass, not a full restart of the original research

## Context

The prior research item established a cross-jurisdiction baseline for Artificial Intelligence regulation in financial services, but this area is moving quickly and can change through new regulator guidance, consultations, implementation timelines, and supervisory statements. This update is needed to answer two practical questions: (1) what is genuinely new since the prior item, and (2) whether the original item missed any material regulator guidance that should have been included.

## Approach

1. Build a dated source timeline from 2026-04-24 onward for each in-scope regulator and identify all newly published or materially updated Artificial Intelligence-related outputs relevant to financial services.
2. Compare each new source against findings in `2026-04-24-ai-agent-regulation-global-financial-services` and classify as: confirms prior conclusion, updates prior conclusion, or introduces net-new obligation/guidance.
3. Run a retrospective gap check on the pre-2026-04-24 window to identify significant sources that existed at the time but were not captured in the prior item.
4. Produce a delta summary with explicit labels: newly issued guidance, changed interpretation, unchanged baseline, and previously missed items.
