# How Can Institutions Align Strategic Relevance Pathways with Human Low-Effort Behaviour?

## Research Question

How can organisations design institutional rules and operating frameworks that structurally reduce the transaction costs of information discovery, so strategic pathways of relevance align with workers' natural low-effort behavioural tendencies?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Integration of thermodynamic, transaction-cost, and institutional perspectives into a unified design model.
- Design principles that jointly minimise effort, preserve relevance, and improve transfer quality.
- Governance and measurement frameworks for sustaining alignment over time.

**Out of scope:**
- Single-team optimisation without organisation-level governance implications.
- Purely theoretical synthesis without actionable design guidance.

**Constraints:** This is a synthesis item that depends on the eight primary questions; final output should include implementable institutional design principles.

## Context

The individual questions in this issue each explain one part of the system. Decision-makers need an integrated model that translates those findings into practical institutional design choices rather than isolated insights.
