# Control deficiencies from bypassing workforce systems of record

## Research Question

What control deficiencies are most common when designated workforce systems of record are bypassed by spreadsheet, presentation, and list-based shadow workflows?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Control design and operating-effectiveness gaps caused by bypass behaviour
- Workforce and skill-tracking process contexts
- Preventive and detective control patterns documented in public frameworks

**Out of scope:**
- Vendor selection decisions
- Enterprise-specific implementation playbooks

**Constraints:** Prioritise framework-backed deficiency patterns and include evidence for prevalence/severity signals.

## Context

Control teams need a concrete deficiency catalog to prioritize remediation and assign ownership.

## Approach

1. Identify recurring bypass patterns and associated control failures.
2. Map failures to control objectives across common governance frameworks.
3. Rank deficiencies by impact on integrity, auditability, and operational resilience.

## Sources

- [ ] [Bank for International Settlements (BIS) Principles for Effective Risk Data Aggregation and Risk Reporting (BCBS 239)](https://www.bis.org/publ/bcbs239.htm) — data-governance expectations
- [ ] [National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) SP 800-53 Rev. 5](https://csrc.nist.gov/pubs/sp/800/53/r5/upd1/final) — control catalog for control-gap mapping
- [ ] [ISACA COBIT 2019 Framework](https://www.isaca.org/resources/cobit) — governance and control objectives
