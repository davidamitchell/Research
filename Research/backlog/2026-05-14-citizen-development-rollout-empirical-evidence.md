# Citizen development rollout empirical evidence

## Research Question

What does peer-reviewed and independently verified empirical evidence reveal about the outcomes, success factors, governance models, and failure modes of citizen development (CD) program rollouts in organisations?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Peer-reviewed studies and independently conducted empirical research (surveys, case studies, longitudinal analyses) on citizen development programs in organisations
- Quantitative and qualitative outcome measures — productivity gains, application quality, time-to-delivery, IT (Information Technology) burden, shadow-IT risk
- Success factors identified across multiple independent studies
- Failure modes and documented risks in CD rollout programs
- Governance structures and their relationship to rollout outcomes
- Scale-up challenges in transitioning from pilot programs to organisation-wide CD rollouts
- Industry surveys with disclosed methodology

**Out of scope:**
- Vendor marketing materials or case studies without independent verification
- General low-code/no-code platform performance benchmarks not tied to CD rollout programs
- Theoretical frameworks without empirical grounding
- Application development methodology comparisons not focused on citizen developers

**Constraints:**
- Prefer peer-reviewed or independently verified studies over vendor-produced content
- Every source listed must include a verifiable URL
- No specific time-horizon restriction, but flag recency risk on fast-moving findings

## Context

Citizen development — the practice of enabling non-IT business users to build applications using low-code/no-code (LCNC) platforms — is widely promoted by vendors and adopted by enterprises. Understanding what empirical evidence says about rollout outcomes is necessary to assess whether and how to pursue such programs, and to identify the governance and risk controls that distinguish successful implementations from those that stall or create technical debt.

## Approach

1. Identify peer-reviewed and independently conducted studies that specifically examine citizen development program rollouts and measure outcomes.
   1a. What quantitative outcomes (productivity gains, application quality, time-to-delivery) have been measured across studies?
   1b. What qualitative outcomes (user satisfaction, IT relationship, governance strain) have been reported?
2. Synthesise the success factors identified empirically across CD rollout studies — what preconditions and in-flight practices correlate with positive outcomes?
3. Document failure modes and risks that have been empirically observed — what causes CD programs to stall, create shadow-IT problems, or generate unsustainable technical debt?
4. Examine how governance models differ between studied implementations and what the evidence says about which governance approaches produce better outcomes.
5. Assess the scale-up evidence — what does research say about the transition from pilot to at-scale CD rollouts, and what inflection points or barriers appear?

## Sources

- [ ] [Sanchis et al. (2020) Low-Code as Enabler of Digital Transformation in Manufacturing Industry](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3417/10/1/12) — empirical analysis of low-code adoption in manufacturing; relevant to outcomes and constraints
- [ ] [Waszkowski (2019) Low-code platform for automating business processes in manufacturing](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2351978919302501) — case study measuring outcomes of non-IT user-built automation
- [ ] [Rokis and Kirikova (2022) Challenges of Low-Code/No-Code Software Development: A Literature Review](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-031-09073-8_54) — systematic review identifying failure modes and governance challenges
- [ ] [Gartner (2021) Innovation Insight for Low-Code Application Platforms](https://www.gartner.com/en/documents/3991199) — Gartner (Analyst Firm) research on LCNC adoption patterns; note vendor-adjacent source
