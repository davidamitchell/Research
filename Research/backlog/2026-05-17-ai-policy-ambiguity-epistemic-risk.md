# Epistemic risks of Artificial Intelligence (AI) policy interpretation

## Research Question

How do Large Language Model (LLM) response style and self-reported confidence affect user risk calibration when interpreting ambiguous policy and compliance requirements?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Measure probability-calibration effects from hedged versus non-hedged AI language in policy guidance contexts
- Test whether syntactic fluency/format quality increases willingness to bypass formal verification channels
- Compare model self-stated confidence against measured interpretation accuracy on ambiguous bylaws and policy excerpts

**Out of scope:**
- Implementing production policy-assistant software
- Entity-specific legal advice
- Exhaustive jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction legal analysis

**Constraints:** Use publicly available sources, distinguish demonstrated evidence from inference, and prioritise sources with explicit governance or empirical grounding.
