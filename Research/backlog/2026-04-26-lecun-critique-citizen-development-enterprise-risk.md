# What does synthesising LeCun's architectural critique of Large Language Models with systems capability debt and citizen development arguments produce as a unified risk framework for regulated financial institutions?

## Research Question

What does the synthesis of Yann LeCun's architectural critique of Large Language Models (LLMs) — no causal world model, no consequence reasoning, verifiable only in formal systems — with the systems capability debt and citizen development argument produce as a unified risk framework for regulated financial institutions; specifically: does LeCun's critique provide theoretical grounding for the claim that citizen development applying LLMs to consequential world actions is not merely a governance risk but an architectural mismatch between tool capability and deployment domain; that the implicit rate-limiting controls removed by agentic AI (human attention, fatigue, working hours) were compensating for exactly the causal reasoning deficit LeCun identifies; that an LLM-based agent acting on an unclassified, ungoverned data estate with incomplete access controls is combining architectural unsuitability with foundational infrastructure failure; and that governance policy expressed in natural language is an insufficient external constraint on a system that processes natural language statistically without causal understanding — meaning formal policy specification is not a governance preference but a structural necessity?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Synthesis of LeCun's architectural critique (Q1) with the systems capability debt and citizen development risk arguments already in this research corpus
- The claim that citizen development applying LLMs to consequential world actions is an architectural mismatch, not merely a governance gap — and the precise technical reasoning that supports or challenges this claim
- The implicit rate-limiting controls removed by agentic AI (human attention, human fatigue, working hours as natural limits on action volume) and whether these controls were compensating for the causal reasoning deficit LeCun identifies
- The compounding risk produced when LLM-based agents operate on an unclassified, ungoverned data estate with incomplete access controls — how architectural unsuitability and infrastructure failure interact
- The claim that natural language governance policy is an insufficient constraint on a system that processes natural language statistically — and what formal policy specification would require to be a structural substitute
- Existing literature on citizen development risk, low-code/no-code governance, and enterprise automation risk in regulated environments

## Context

The Q1 and Q2 items establish LeCun's architectural critique and the verifiability asymmetry respectively. This item (Q3) performs the synthesis: applying those technical foundations to the specific enterprise risk scenario of citizen development in regulated financial services. The output is a unified risk framework that connects architectural theory (LeCun), deployment pattern (citizen development), infrastructure state (systems capability debt, ungoverned data estates), and governance mechanism (natural language policy versus formal specification). This framework is the analytical foundation for the investment case in Q4 — it defines what is actually at risk and why, before Q4 asks what should be done about it.

## Approach

1. **Corpus audit:** Identify the specific research items and documents in this corpus that contain the systems capability debt argument and the citizen development risk argument. Establish the specific claims from each that are relevant to the synthesis.
2. **Architectural mismatch test:** Apply LeCun's architectural critique to the citizen development deployment pattern — does it provide theoretical grounding for characterising citizen development applying LLMs to consequential world actions as an architectural mismatch (tool not capable of the task) rather than merely a governance gap (tool capable but inadequately controlled)?
3. **Rate-limiting control analysis:** Enumerate the implicit controls that human actors performing the same work would have provided (attention limits, fatigue, working hours, judgement, contextual awareness) and assess whether these controls were compensating for the specific deficit LeCun identifies — absence of causal reasoning and consequence modelling. This is an inferential claim; assess its strength carefully.
4. **Infrastructure compounding analysis:** Characterise the compounding risk produced when architectural unsuitability (LLM acting in a domain without causal reasoning) combines with infrastructure failure (unclassified data, ungoverned access, incomplete audit trail) — is this additive risk, multiplicative risk, or a qualitatively different risk category?
5. **Natural language policy constraint analysis:** Assess the claim that natural language governance policy is structurally insufficient as a constraint on a system that processes natural language statistically — what would formal policy specification require, and what evidence exists that natural language policies are systematically misinterpreted or ignored by LLM agents?
6. **Unified risk framework construction:** Synthesise the above into a structured risk framework covering: (a) architectural mismatch layer, (b) removed compensating controls layer, (c) infrastructure compounding layer, (d) governance constraint failure layer — with explicit claims, evidence, and confidence levels for each.
