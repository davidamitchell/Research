# Key-person dependency and Basel execution, delivery, and process-management risk linkage

## Research Question

How should key-person dependency in workforce-critical processes be mapped to execution, delivery, and process-management risk categories in Basel Committee framing?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Key-person dependency risk mechanisms in operational processes
- Basel-compatible classification and reporting language
- Escalation triggers and control expectations

**Out of scope:**
- Individual performance management practices
- Enterprise-specific organizational redesign

**Constraints:** Prioritize primary Basel sources and ensure classification guidance is explicit and auditable.

## Context

Organizations often treat key-person dependency as a staffing issue rather than a formal risk category, reducing control visibility.

## Approach

1. Extract Basel language relevant to concentration and execution dependency risk.
2. Define mapping rules from key-person dependency patterns to Basel risk categories.
3. Identify control and governance actions implied by each mapped category.

## Sources

- [ ] [Bank for International Settlements (BIS) Basel III reforms](https://www.bis.org/bcbs/publ/d424.htm) — supervisory framing
- [ ] [Bank for International Settlements (BIS) Principles for Operational Resilience](https://www.bis.org/bcbs/publ/d516.htm) — dependency and continuity expectations
- [ ] [Bank for International Settlements (BIS) Principles for Effective Risk Data Aggregation and Risk Reporting (BCBS 239)](https://www.bis.org/publ/bcbs239.htm) — governance and reporting implications
