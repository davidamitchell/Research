# How should banks govern micro-agent sprawl and bottleneck shifts across divisions?

## Research Question

How does uncoordinated growth of department-level micro-agents change systemic risk and reporting integrity in banks, and which governance architecture can maintain cross-agent consistency, lineage, and operational resilience as bottlenecks shift from data entry to compliance decision queues?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Cross-division inconsistencies caused by local agent deployment in retail, commercial, and risk functions.
- Governance requirements for model and agent lineage, version control, and contradiction detection.
- Operational bottleneck migration under increased technical throughput.
- Mapping to Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA), Federal Reserve model risk guidance, and ISO/IEC 42001 governance controls.

**Out of scope:**
- Vendor-specific product comparisons unless directly tied to governance capability.
- Non-financial sectors.

**Constraints:** Emphasise measurable governance mechanisms that can be stress-tested and audited.

## Context

As agent usage decentralises, failure risk can emerge from coordination gaps rather than model quality alone, making enterprise-level governance and resilience design a prerequisite for trustworthy deployment.

## Approach

1. Characterise organizational failure modes created by distributed agent adoption and local optimisation.
2. Identify stress-testing and systems-modelling methods that capture bottleneck migration effects.
3. Evaluate governance frameworks for lineage, control-plane coordination, and contradiction management.
4. Produce an implementation-oriented governance blueprint for regulated banking environments.
