# Which Network Structures Bottleneck or Accelerate Knowledge Flow?

## Research Question

Which social-network topologies concentrate knowledge flow into fragile bottlenecks, and which topologies enable fast cross-boundary transfer of tacit knowledge without overloading central actors?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Roles of central connectors, brokers, and boundary spanners in internal knowledge flow.
- Structural indicators of bottleneck risk and resilience.
- Comparison of siloed versus cross-cutting network designs.

**Out of scope:**
- Technical network infrastructure performance (servers, bandwidth, latency).
- External market network effects between firms.

**Constraints:** Focus on organisational network analysis evidence with transferable structural metrics.

## Context

Organisation charts describe authority, not actual knowledge flow. Mapping structural flow dynamics is a prerequisite for deciding whether to strengthen central hubs or deliberately redistribute knowledge-transfer load.

## Approach

1. Characterise network roles that increase throughput versus those that create queueing bottlenecks.
2. Compare outcomes in high-centrality versus distributed knowledge networks.
3. Translate findings into design metrics usable in organisational diagnostics.

## Sources

- [ ] [Granovetter, M. S. (1973) The Strength of Weak Ties](https://doi.org/10.1086/225469) — classic theory on bridging ties and diffusion across groups.
- [ ] [Burt, R. S. (2004) Structural Holes and Good Ideas](https://doi.org/10.1086/421787) — evidence that brokerage positions can accelerate novel knowledge transfer.
