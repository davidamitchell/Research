# Enterprise risk and workforce management: formal risk classification and controls for shadow systems bypassing systems of record

## Research Question

How should enterprise risk and control frameworks formally classify and mitigate the risk created when business-critical workforce data is maintained in unmanaged shadow tools and manual workarounds instead of a designated system of record?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Basel III and other Basel Committee on Banking Supervision (BCBS) operational-risk and execution-risk framing relevant to process management and key-person dependency
- International Organization for Standardization (ISO) 31000 risk-management principles and taxonomy mapping
- National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) control frameworks, including NIST Special Publication (SP) 800-53 information-integrity requirements
- Control-deficiency patterns when formal systems of record (for example Cornerstone) are bypassed by spreadsheets, presentation decks, and list-based shadow tooling
- Process-Risk-Control (PRC) scoring implications when workforce and skill-tracking processes are undocumented or unstandardized

**Out of scope:**
- Vendor product comparison or procurement recommendation for workforce-management software
- Internal implementation design for any single enterprise environment
- Jurisdiction-specific legal advice beyond what is explicitly defined in publicly available framework documents

**Constraints:**
- Prioritise primary framework sources (standards bodies, Basel publications, and official control-framework references)
- Distinguish process inefficiency from systemic latent risk using explicit taxonomy criteria
- Expand acronyms on first use in findings

## Context

Enterprises frequently treat spreadsheet-driven workforce tracking as a local productivity issue, but the same behaviour may represent a formally classifiable control failure, latent operational risk, and information-integrity weakness under major risk frameworks. A consolidated mapping is needed so risk, audit, and workforce-management functions can use shared language when classifying severity, assigning controls, and prioritising remediation.

## Approach

1. Map how Basel III, International Organization for Standardization (ISO) 31000, and National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) frameworks classify the issue of business-critical data held in unmanaged spreadsheets and presentation decks.
2. Identify control deficiencies associated with bypassing designated systems of record (for example Cornerstone) through manual workforce-management workarounds.
3. Define taxonomy criteria that separate process inefficiency from systemic latent risk in human-capital and skill-tracking workflows.
4. Extract the structural requirements for a defined process under Control Objectives for Information and Related Technologies (COBIT) 2019 and Capability Maturity Model Integration (CMMI) before risk mitigation can be considered effective.
5. Assess how missing data provenance in Microsoft (MS) Lists, Excel, and PowerPoint (PPTX) artifacts conflicts with NIST SP 800-53 information-integrity controls.
6. Evaluate inherent-risk score impacts in a PRC library when underlying workforce processes are unstandardized or undocumented.
7. Investigate how workforce-capacity and skill-taxonomy structures can integrate with a Policy-LSP framework to detect capability debt automatically.
8. Clarify the relationship between key-person dependency and execution, delivery, and process-management risk in Basel Committee framing.
