# RQ 1.1 — Formalising Popper's Falsifiability as a Criterion Between Mechanism and Interpolation

## Research Question

How can Karl Popper's criterion of demarcation and falsifiability be mathematically formalised to distinguish between a model that explains a physical mechanism and one that merely interpolates observational data?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Mathematical formalisation of Popper's falsifiability criterion in the context of statistical learning theory
- Relationship between Vapnik-Chervonenkis (VC) Dimension and logical content
- Kolmogorov Complexity as an operationalisation of Occam's Razor
- Distinguishing explanatory models from purely interpolative curve-fitting models

**Out of scope:**
- Sociological or historical analyses of Popper's influence
- Applications outside of machine learning and physical modelling

**Constraints:** Focus on formal, quantitative treatments wherever possible; philosophical treatments are supporting material only.

## Context

The ability to distinguish a model that captures a true physical mechanism from one that merely fits observed data is the foundational epistemological question underlying all of machine learning. As predictive models grow in complexity, the risk of mistaking interpolation for explanation grows. Popper's falsifiability criterion provides a starting framework, but it requires mathematical operationalisation to be usable in contemporary machine learning contexts. This item opens the dependency chain: all subsequent phases (2–6) rest on understanding the boundary between genuine explanatory power and curve-fitting.

## Approach

1. Review Popper's original demarcation criterion and its logical content formulation.
2. Map falsifiability onto VC Dimension: how does a model with higher VC Dimension score on Popperian falsifiability?
3. Examine Kolmogorov Complexity as a formal proxy for Occam's Razor and its relationship to minimal description length.
4. Construct a formal framework distinguishing mechanistic models (low Kolmogorov complexity relative to predictive accuracy) from interpolative models (high VC Dimension, low logical content).
5. Identify where modern deep learning sits in this framework.

## Sources

- [ ] [Popper, K. (1959) The Logic of Scientific Discovery](https://archive.org/details/logicofscientifi0000popp) — original demarcation criterion and falsifiability formulation
- [ ] [Vapnik, V. N. (1998) Statistical Learning Theory](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Statistical+Learning+Theory-p-9780471030034) — VC Dimension and empirical risk minimisation foundations
- [ ] [Rissanen, J. (1978) Modelling by shortest data description — Automatica](https://doi.org/10.1016/0005-1098(78)90005-5) — Minimum Description Length as a formalisation of Occam's Razor
- [ ] [Li, M. & Vitányi, P. (2008) An Introduction to Kolmogorov Complexity and Its Applications](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-0-387-49820-1) — Kolmogorov Complexity as a formal complexity measure
