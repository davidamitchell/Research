# Visibility and exit outcomes: vendor-supplied versus internally governed do-mode automation

## Research Question

How often does vendor-supplied do-mode automation produce materially worse visibility and exit outcomes than internally governed do-mode automation?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Comparative evidence on observability, auditability, portability, and contractual exit readiness between vendor-supplied and internally governed automation
- Definitions of materially worse outcomes with measurable thresholds or proxy metrics
- Enterprise contexts where do-mode automation spans workflow orchestration, robotic process automation, and agent operations

**Out of scope:**
- Legal advice on drafting enterprise contracts
- Product-by-product feature scorecards detached from governance and exit evidence

**Constraints:** Prioritise studies, guidance, or post-incident analyses that include explicit exit, lock-in, or visibility evidence rather than opinion-only commentary.

## Context

Sourcing strategy and governance controls depend on whether externally supplied automation systematically weakens observability or reversibility relative to internal governance.

## Approach

1. Define measurable visibility and exit outcome criteria applicable to both vendor-supplied and internally governed automation.
2. Collect comparative evidence from standards, incidents, and enterprise case studies that report those criteria.
3. Estimate relative frequency or directional prevalence of worse outcomes and identify confidence limits.
