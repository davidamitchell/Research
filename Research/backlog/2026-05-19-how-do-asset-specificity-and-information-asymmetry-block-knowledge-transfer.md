# How Do Asset Specificity and Information Asymmetry Block Knowledge Transfer?

## Research Question

How do highly specific expert knowledge assets create information asymmetries that raise verification costs and exceed absorptive capacity, causing seekers to abandon high-value knowledge pathways?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Asset specificity in expert knowledge and its transfer implications.
- Information asymmetry between experts and non-experts.
- Verification burden and absorptive-capacity constraints on seekers.

**Out of scope:**
- Technical knowledge-representation standards for databases.
- Skill-assessment frameworks for hiring and promotion.

**Constraints:** Prioritise empirical and theory-grounded work linking asset specificity, asymmetry, and absorptive capacity.

## Context

High-value expertise often remains trapped because seekers cannot evaluate or absorb it quickly enough. Understanding this failure mode is necessary for designing institutions that preserve depth while remaining usable by non-specialists.

## Approach

1. Define how asset specificity appears in internal expert knowledge domains.
2. Analyse how asymmetry and verification complexity affect seeker behaviour.
3. Evaluate mechanisms that improve transfer without oversimplifying specialist knowledge.

## Sources

- [ ] [Williamson, O. E. (1985) The Economic Institutions of Capitalism](https://archive.org/details/economicinstitut00will) — canonical treatment of asset specificity and governance implications.
- [ ] [Cohen, W. M. & Levinthal, D. A. (1990) Absorptive Capacity: A New Perspective on Learning and Innovation](https://doi.org/10.2307/2393553) — foundational model for knowledge absorption limits.
