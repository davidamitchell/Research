# Synthesis: what is the complete framework for formulating high-quality research questions?

## Research Question

Integrating findings from the five primary research items on question quality, number and scope, question types, common mistakes, and development techniques, what is the complete, actionable framework a researcher should follow to move from a broad topic to a set of high-quality, answerable research questions — and what are the critical decision points and failure modes at each stage?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Integration and cross-referencing of findings from all five primary items in this series:
  - `2026-05-01-qualities-effective-research-questions` — what makes a question well-formed
  - `2026-05-01-number-scope-research-questions` — how many questions a project needs and their relation to aims and objectives
  - `2026-05-01-types-research-questions` — question typology and design implications
  - `2026-05-01-common-mistakes-research-questions` — failure modes and corrections
  - `2026-05-01-developing-sharpening-research-questions` — development templates and iteration techniques
- A unified, stage-by-stage framework that sequences the insights from all five items into a single coherent process
- Identification of cross-cutting themes, tensions, and gaps that only emerge when the five items are read together
- A consolidated checklist and decision guide usable by a researcher at the start of any project

**Out of scope:**
- Primary investigation of any topic already covered in the five source items — this is a synthesis, not a new primary investigation
- Domain-specific research question conventions beyond what is covered in the primary items
- Research design choices downstream of question formulation (sampling, data collection, analysis)

**Constraints:**
- This item must not be started until all five primary items (`blocks` list) are completed
- Claims in the synthesis must be traceable back to specific findings in the primary items — no new claims that are not grounded in the primary research
- The synthesis should surface tensions between primary items (e.g., where quality criteria and development templates pull in different directions)

## Context

The five primary items in this series each address one dimension of research question formulation from the Grad Coach talk (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QT7W_uHjqWE) and supporting methodology literature. Individually, they are useful reference items. Together, they form the components of a complete framework — but the integration work has not yet been done. This synthesis item exists to do that integration: to identify the logical sequence, the decision points, the failure modes at each stage, and the cross-cutting principles that apply throughout. The expected output is a single, concise, actionable guide that subsumes all five primary items into a practitioner-ready resource.

## Approach

1. **Read and extract key findings** — For each of the five primary items, extract the key findings and the practical outputs (checklists, typologies, templates, decision frameworks). Note the form of the output (checklist, decision tree, typology, technique catalogue).

2. **Identify a natural sequence** — Is there a logical order in which the five dimensions should be applied? Does a researcher need to understand question types (typology) before selecting development techniques, or after? Map the dependencies and produce a recommended sequence.

3. **Identify cross-cutting themes** — What principles apply across all five dimensions? (e.g., "specificity" appears in both quality criteria and common-mistakes literature.) Consolidate these into a short set of universal principles.

4. **Identify tensions and trade-offs** — Where do the five dimensions pull in different directions? (e.g., template-driven question generation may bias toward certain question types; the quality criterion of specificity may conflict with exploratory research designs that require openness.) Document these tensions explicitly.

5. **Construct the integrated framework** — Produce a stage-by-stage framework:
   - Stage 1: Select question type (informed by `2026-05-01-types-research-questions`)
   - Stage 2: Apply a development technique to generate a draft question (informed by `2026-05-01-developing-sharpening-research-questions`)
   - Stage 3: Evaluate against quality criteria (informed by `2026-05-01-qualities-effective-research-questions`)
   - Stage 4: Check for common failure modes and apply corrections (informed by `2026-05-01-common-mistakes-research-questions`)
   - Stage 5: Set the question count and map to aims and objectives (informed by `2026-05-01-number-scope-research-questions`)

6. **Produce the consolidated output** — A concise practitioner guide (a single Markdown document) combining the sequence, universal principles, decision points, and consolidated checklist.
