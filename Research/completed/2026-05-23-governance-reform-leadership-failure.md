# Barriers to governance reform, leadership failure modes, and reform mechanisms in regulated enterprises

## Research Question

What institutional and organisational barriers prevent effective governance reform in regulated enterprises, through what leadership failure modes are dysfunctional controls perpetuated, and by what mechanisms have successful reforms been achieved?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Institutional barriers to governance change: path dependence, meaning earlier institutional choices constrain later reform, sunk costs in existing governance infrastructure, and regulatory lock-in
- Organisational barriers: principal-agent (PA) problems between boards and executives, executives and governance functions, and governance functions and front-line staff
- Leadership failure modes: diffusion of accountability, visible control activity that transfers blame or signals diligence without materially reducing the underlying risk, short-tenure incentive misalignment, and regulatory capture at board level
- Mechanisms of successful reform: structured simplification, controls judged by the risk they reduce rather than by the paperwork they produce, regulatory dialogue, and governance assurance frameworks
- Empirical evidence from regulated enterprises that have reformed governance and the conditions that made reform possible

**Out of scope:**
- Regulatory reform (focus is on internal governance, not changes to external regulatory frameworks)
- Change management methodology as a general discipline (only where directly applied to governance reform)
- Technology-specific governance reforms (unless illustrative of broader institutional mechanisms)

**Constraints:** Prefer evidence from regulated enterprises in financial services, healthcare, and energy. Include public-sector cases where they exhibit regulated-enterprise characteristics (accountability structures, compliance obligations, audit regimes).

## Context

Regulated enterprises often recognise governance failure yet still struggle to remove inherited control layers, because institutional persistence and coordination frictions make subtraction politically harder than addition. [inference; source: https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511808678; https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/-/media/boe/files/news/2012/august/the-dog-and-the-frisbee-paper-by-andy-haldane.pdf; https://www.apra.gov.au/news-and-publications/apra-releases-cba-prudential-inquiry-final-report-and-accepts-enforceable]

Adjacent completed items in this corpus already establish when controls remain enabling and how they degrade into bureaucracy, so this item focuses on why leaders do not fix known problems and which mechanisms have actually broken that stalemate. [fact; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-23-governance-controls-effectiveness-conditions.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-23-governance-failure-mechanisms-bureaucracy-circumvention.html]

## Approach

1. Identify and classify institutional barriers to governance reform: (a) path dependence and sunk costs in existing governance structures, (b) regulatory lock-in and regulator risk-aversion, (c) information asymmetry between governance designers and governed parties, (d) vested interests in existing control and assurance structures.
2. Map leadership failure modes that perpetuate governance dysfunction: (a) accountability diffusion across committee structures, (b) short-tenure incentive misalignment, (c) visible compliance activity used as regulatory risk transfer rather than risk reduction, (d) regulatory capture at board or governance committee level, (e) failure to distinguish compliance cost from control effectiveness.
3. Identify the principal-agent (PA) problems that sustain each failure mode: who holds the information, who bears the cost, and who benefits from the status quo?
4. Review case studies of successful governance reform: what institutional conditions, leadership actions, and reform sequencing enabled change? What counterfactual pressures preserved the status quo until change became unavoidable?
5. Synthesise into a set of actionable reform enablers, conditions and mechanisms that practitioners and regulators can use to catalyse governance improvement without adding governance overhead.

#### A. Institutional barriers to reform

- [fact; source: https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511808678] The Cambridge summary of North's book presents institutions as arrangements that structure interaction under uncertainty and describes institutional development as path dependent, which supports the claim that inherited governance arrangements can persist even when they are no longer efficient.
- [inference; source: https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511808678; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-03-10-nature-of-the-firm-coase-organisations.html] In governance reform, path dependence means older approval layers, committees, and assurance routines survive because removing them requires concentrated political responsibility, while leaving them in place spreads cost diffusely across the organisation.
- [fact; source: https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/-/media/boe/files/news/2012/august/the-dog-and-the-frisbee-paper-by-andy-haldane.pdf] Haldane and Madouros argue that complex rules have high information-collection and processing costs, rely on over-fitted models, and can induce people to manage to the rules rather than to the underlying objective.
- [inference; source: https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/-/media/boe/files/news/2012/august/the-dog-and-the-frisbee-paper-by-andy-haldane.pdf; https://www.fsb.org/2017/04/thematic-review-on-corporate-governance/] Complex governance architectures therefore become self-protecting barriers to reform, because they are expensive to understand, hard to challenge in detail, and easy to defend as prudence even when they degrade decision quality.

#### B. Leadership failure modes that perpetuate dysfunctional controls

- [fact; source: https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/government/uploads/system/uploads/attachment_data/file/279124/0947.pdf] Francis says Mid Staffordshire's provider Trust Board failed to listen sufficiently to patients and staff, failed to correct known deficiencies, and tolerated a negative culture with poor standards and disengagement from leadership responsibilities.
- [fact; source: https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/government/uploads/system/uploads/attachment_data/file/279124/0947.pdf] Francis also identifies assumptions that monitoring, performance management, or intervention were someone else's responsibility, which is direct evidence of accountability diffusion across oversight bodies.
- [fact; source: https://www.apra.gov.au/news-and-publications/apra-releases-cba-prudential-inquiry-final-report-and-accepts-enforceable] The Australian Prudential Regulation Authority (APRA) inquiry into the Commonwealth Bank of Australia (CBA) found inadequate Board and committee challenge of emerging non-financial risks, unclear accountabilities, weak escalation, overly complex and bureaucratic decision-making, and a remuneration framework that had little sting when poor risk or customer outcomes materialised.
- [fact; source: https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-OILCOMMISSION/pdf/GPO-OILCOMMISSION.pdf; https://www.boem.gov/about-boem/regulations-guidance/regulatory-reforms] The Deepwater Horizon commission report says the disaster exposed the costs of a culture of complacency, repeated missed warning signals, failure to share information, and insufficient commitment to safety from the highest management levels down, while the Bureau of Ocean Energy Management (BOEM) says the post-disaster response required structural regulatory reorganisation and new accountability mechanisms.
- [inference; source: https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/government/uploads/system/uploads/attachment_data/file/279124/0947.pdf; https://www.apra.gov.au/news-and-publications/apra-releases-cba-prudential-inquiry-final-report-and-accepts-enforceable; https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-OILCOMMISSION/pdf/GPO-OILCOMMISSION.pdf] Across sectors, leadership failure is less often total absence of governance than selective attention: boards and executives continue to receive reports, but they overweight target progress, financial success, or procedural completion relative to weak signals about underlying harm.

#### C. Principal-agent structure sustaining the failure modes

- [inference; source: https://www.apra.gov.au/news-and-publications/apra-releases-cba-prudential-inquiry-final-report-and-accepts-enforceable; https://www.federalreserve.gov/supervisionreg/srletters/SR2103.htm; https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/government/uploads/system/uploads/attachment_data/file/279124/0947.pdf] The recurring principal-agent structure is that boards depend on management-filtered information, management depends on business lines for execution, and control functions often have visibility without full authority, so the actors best able to observe deterioration are not always the actors able to authorise reform.
- [inference; source: https://www.apra.gov.au/news-and-publications/apra-releases-cba-prudential-inquiry-final-report-and-accepts-enforceable; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-23-governance-failure-mechanisms-bureaucracy-circumvention.html] Business lines often keep the upside from speed, sales, or reduced friction, while governance and risk functions bear the cost of escalation and challenge, which biases the system toward preserving cumbersome but familiar controls until an external shock changes the payoff structure.
- [inference; source: https://www.federalreserve.gov/supervisionreg/srletters/SR2103.htm; https://www.bis.org/bcbs/publ/d328.htm] The Federal Reserve and Basel guidance both respond to this information problem by requiring boards to focus on core oversight, direct management on information needs, and support independent risk management and internal audit, which implies that reform depends on redesigning information rights as much as decision rights.

#### D. Mechanisms of successful reform

- [fact; source: https://www.fsb.org/2017/04/thematic-review-on-corporate-governance/; https://www.bis.org/bcbs/publ/d328.htm; https://www.federalreserve.gov/supervisionreg/srletters/SR2103.htm] Financial-sector supervisory guidance converges on a repeated reform pattern: clearer division of responsibilities, proportionality to size and risk, regular board-effectiveness assessment, stronger independent control functions, and better board information for oversight.
- [fact; source: https://www.apra.gov.au/news-and-publications/apra-releases-cba-prudential-inquiry-final-report-and-accepts-enforceable; https://www.apra.gov.au/news-and-publications/apra-removes-cba%E2%80%99s-operational-risk-capital-add-on] APRA paired its CBA findings with an Enforceable Undertaking, a capital add-on, and multi-year validation, then removed the remaining capital add-on only after concluding the reforms were completed, sustainable, and well-embedded.
- [fact; source: https://www.boem.gov/about-boem/regulations-guidance/regulatory-reforms; https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-OILCOMMISSION/pdf/GPO-OILCOMMISSION.pdf] After Deepwater Horizon, the United States split the former Minerals Management Service into three agencies with clearly defined roles, added an investigations and review unit, and introduced conflict-of-interest and review reforms, which is a concrete example of structural simplification plus accountability clarification following failure.
- [fact; source: https://www.bmj.com/content/380/bmj.p513; https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/government/uploads/system/uploads/attachment_data/file/279124/0947.pdf] Post-Francis healthcare reforms included a statutory duty of candour, Freedom to Speak Up guardians, and inspection changes oriented toward openness, but the later British Medical Journal (BMJ) review says sustainable improvement depends on organisations actually listening, learning, and maintaining strong leadership rather than merely enacting the formal recommendations.
- [inference; source: https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/-/media/boe/files/news/2012/august/the-dog-and-the-frisbee-paper-by-andy-haldane.pdf; https://www.federalreserve.gov/supervisionreg/srletters/SR2103.htm; https://www.apra.gov.au/news-and-publications/apra-releases-cba-prudential-inquiry-final-report-and-accepts-enforceable] Successful reform mechanisms are therefore not simply "more controls"; they are selective simplification, tighter ownership of non-financial risk, better challenge capacity, and repeated validation that the new structure changes outcomes rather than only documentation.

#### E. Cross-sector synthesis

- [inference; source: https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/government/uploads/system/uploads/attachment_data/file/279124/0947.pdf; https://www.apra.gov.au/news-and-publications/apra-releases-cba-prudential-inquiry-final-report-and-accepts-enforceable; https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-OILCOMMISSION/pdf/GPO-OILCOMMISSION.pdf] The common cross-sector barrier is not lack of formal governance architecture but misalignment between who sees early warning signals, who bears the immediate cost of action, and who is accountable for intervening.
- [inference; source: https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/-/media/boe/files/news/2012/august/the-dog-and-the-frisbee-paper-by-andy-haldane.pdf; https://www.apra.gov.au/news-and-publications/apra-releases-cba-prudential-inquiry-final-report-and-accepts-enforceable; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-23-governance-controls-effectiveness-conditions.html] Complexity is a recurrent reform barrier because it obscures ownership, raises review cost, and lets organisations defend low-signal control activity as diligence.
- [inference; source: https://www.apra.gov.au/news-and-publications/apra-removes-cba%E2%80%99s-operational-risk-capital-add-on; https://www.boem.gov/about-boem/regulations-guidance/regulatory-reforms; https://www.bmj.com/content/380/bmj.p513] Durable reform often follows a forcing event and then requires multi-year reinforcement through capital consequences, structural reorganisation, or continuing external review rather than a single declaration of cultural change.

### Executive Summary

Governance reform in regulated enterprises is usually blocked by institutional lock-in and incentive asymmetry rather than by ignorance alone. [inference; source: https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511808678; https://www.apra.gov.au/news-and-publications/apra-releases-cba-prudential-inquiry-final-report-and-accepts-enforceable; https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/government/uploads/system/uploads/attachment_data/file/279124/0947.pdf]

Recurring leadership failure modes are accountability diffusion, filtered escalation, target or success bias, and tolerance of complex control structures that work better on paper than in practice. [fact; source: https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/government/uploads/system/uploads/attachment_data/file/279124/0947.pdf; https://www.apra.gov.au/news-and-publications/apra-releases-cba-prudential-inquiry-final-report-and-accepts-enforceable; https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GPO-OILCOMMISSION/pdf/GPO-OILCOMMISSION.pdf]

Successful reforms recur around the same mechanisms: simplify the architecture, clarify ownership, strengthen independent challenge, improve board information, and keep external pressure in place until the new behaviour is embedded. [inference; source: https://www.fsb.org/2017/04/thematic-review-on-corporate-governance/; https://www.federalreserve.gov/supervisionreg/srletters/SR2103.htm; https://www.apra.gov.au/news-and-publications/apra-removes-cba%E2%80%99s-operational-risk-capital-add-on]

Across the cases and guidance reviewed here, durable reform is associated with redesigns of information rights, challenge rights, and review cadence rather than with additional layers of visible-but-low-signal compliance work. [inference; source: https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/-/media/boe/files/news/2012/august/the-dog-and-the-frisbee-paper-by-andy-haldane.pdf; https://www.bmj.com/content/380/bmj.p513; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-23-governance-failure-mechanisms-bureaucracy-circumvention.html]
