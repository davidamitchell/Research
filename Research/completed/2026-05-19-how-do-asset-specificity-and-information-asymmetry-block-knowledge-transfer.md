# How Do Asset Specificity and Information Asymmetry Block Knowledge Transfer?

## Research Question

How do highly specific expert knowledge assets, knowledge investments whose value depends heavily on a particular context or relationship, create information asymmetries, situations where experts know materially more than seekers, that raise verification costs and exceed absorptive capacity, the seeker's ability to recognize, assimilate, and apply external knowledge, causing seekers to abandon high-value knowledge pathways?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Asset specificity in expert knowledge and its transfer implications.
- Information asymmetry between experts and non-experts.
- Verification burden and absorptive-capacity constraints on seekers.
- Transfer mechanisms that preserve specialist depth while improving usability.

**Out of scope:**
- Technical knowledge-representation standards for databases.
- Skill-assessment frameworks for hiring and promotion.
- A full welfare analysis of intellectual-property policy.

**Constraints:** Prioritise empirical and theory-grounded work linking asset specificity, asymmetry, causal ambiguity, uncertainty about which elements make knowledge work, and absorptive capacity.

## Context

High-value expertise often remains trapped because seekers cannot cheaply judge whether the knowledge is worth acquiring, cannot easily translate it into their own context, and may need the expert's help to know whether they understood it correctly. [inference; source: https://www.nber.org/system/files/chapters/c2144/c2144.pdf; https://eric.ed.gov/?id=EJ406851; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105]
That failure mode matters for organisational design because firms often respond by internalising specialist capabilities, relying on long-lived relationships, or accepting key-person concentration instead of treating expert knowledge as a frictionless market good. [inference; source: https://github.com/davidamitchell/Research/blob/main/Research/completed/2026-03-10-nature-of-the-firm-coase-organisations.md; https://github.com/davidamitchell/Research/blob/main/Research/completed/2026-05-09-key-person-dependency-basel-risk-linkage.md]

## Approach

1. Define how asset specificity appears in internal expert knowledge domains.
2. Analyse how asymmetry, causal ambiguity, and verification complexity affect seeker behaviour.
3. Evaluate mechanisms that improve transfer without oversimplifying specialist knowledge.

## Research Skill Output

### §0 Initialise

- Question: How do highly specific expert knowledge assets create information asymmetries, verification burdens, and absorptive-capacity limits that cause seekers to abandon valuable knowledge pathways?
- Scope: explain the mechanism, identify where transfer breaks, and evaluate transfer designs that reduce abandonment without flattening specialist knowledge into misleading simplifications.
- Constraints: public sources only, explicit fact versus inference marking, Uniform Resource Locator (URL)-backed citations only, and mirrored synthesis between §6 and Findings.
- Output format: structured research item with Executive Summary, Key Findings, Evidence Map, Assumptions, Analysis, Risks, Gaps, Uncertainties, Open Questions, and Output.

### §1 Question Decomposition

- [fact] Root question decomposed into five branches.

**Branch A, what makes expert knowledge asset-specific?**
- A1: What does asset specificity mean when the asset is expert knowledge rather than machinery?
- A2: Why does specificity make the knowledge harder to price, verify, or redeploy?

**Branch B, where does information asymmetry enter?**
- B1: Why can seekers not fully know the value of expert knowledge before disclosure?
- B2: Why do credentials and reputation only partly solve that problem?

**Branch C, when does transfer fail on the recipient side?**
- C1: What is absorptive capacity?
- C2: How do causal ambiguity, locality, and tacitness raise the recipient's learning burden?

**Branch D, why do seekers abandon the pathway?**
- D1: Which mechanisms make the expected verification and learning cost exceed the expected benefit?
- D2: Which role does relationship quality play?

**Branch E, what improves transfer without destroying depth?**
- E1: Which transfer methods reduce ambiguity or increase absorptive capacity?
- E2: Which methods merely translate enough shared context to let specialists and non-specialists coordinate?

### §2 Investigation

#### A. Asset specificity and expert knowledge

- [fact; source: https://github.com/davidamitchell/Research/blob/main/Research/completed/2026-03-10-nature-of-the-firm-coase-organisations.md] The prior completed transaction-cost item synthesises Williamson's core claim that high asset specificity makes parties vulnerable to hold-up and therefore increases the appeal of hierarchy or other tighter governance forms.
- [inference; source: https://github.com/davidamitchell/Research/blob/main/Research/completed/2026-03-10-nature-of-the-firm-coase-organisations.md; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1108%2Fjkm-08-2020-0590] In expert knowledge domains, the asset-specific investment is often a human one: context-laden know-how, local problem histories, and firm-specific interpretation routines whose value falls outside the focal setting, and deeper knowledge flows can themselves increase that specificity.
- [fact; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1108%2Fjkm-08-2020-0590] Zheng et al. report that coupled and outbound knowledge flows have a direct positive effect on asset specificity in their open-innovation sample, which implies that the organisational investments that make collaboration productive can also make the knowledge less generic and more relationship-bound.

#### B. Information asymmetry and verification cost

- [fact; source: https://www.nber.org/system/files/chapters/c2144/c2144.pdf] Arrow states that information has a fundamental demand paradox: its value for the purchaser is not known until the purchaser has the information, but then the purchaser has in effect acquired it without cost.
- [inference; source: https://www.nber.org/system/files/chapters/c2144/c2144.pdf] Expert knowledge therefore creates an ex ante information asymmetry for the seeker, because the seeker needs some disclosure, demonstration, or expert interpretation to judge value, but that very process begins to transfer the knowledge and so raises verification cost for both sides.
- [inference; source: https://www.nber.org/system/files/chapters/c2144/c2144.pdf; https://github.com/davidamitchell/Research/blob/main/Research/completed/2026-03-10-nature-of-the-firm-coase-organisations.md] That asymmetry helps explain why markets for highly specific expertise rely heavily on hierarchy, repeated relationships, and reputation, rather than on one-shot spot verification.

#### C. Absorptive capacity and stickiness

- [fact; source: https://eric.ed.gov/?id=EJ406851] Cohen and Levinthal define absorptive capacity as the ability of a firm to recognize the value of new external information, assimilate it, and apply it to commercial ends, and they argue that this capacity is largely a function of prior related knowledge.
- [fact; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105] Szulanski reports from 271 observations of 122 best-practice transfers in eight companies that the major barriers to internal knowledge transfer are knowledge-related factors, especially the recipient's lack of absorptive capacity, causal ambiguity, and an arduous relationship between source and recipient.
- [fact; source: https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/journal-of-management-and-organization/article/abs/determinants-of-causal-ambiguity-and-difficulty-of-knowledge-transfer-within-the-firm/C55CBA4022809A31A9F9D445591A7C67] Uygur identifies four antecedents of causal ambiguity, complexity, tacitness, relevance to the existing knowledge base, and locality of knowledge.
- [inference; source: https://eric.ed.gov/?id=EJ406851; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105; https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/journal-of-management-and-organization/article/abs/determinants-of-causal-ambiguity-and-difficulty-of-knowledge-transfer-within-the-firm/C55CBA4022809A31A9F9D445591A7C67] A seeker abandons the pathway when the knowledge requires more prior related knowledge than the seeker possesses, because the seeker cannot reliably recognize which parts matter, cannot assimilate them into an existing frame, and cannot tell whether apparent progress is genuine understanding.

#### D. Tacitness, locality, and measurement difficulty

- [fact; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7645890/] Rosellini and Hawamdeh argue that a central challenge is not acquiring and storing information but transforming it into useful knowledge and measuring that transfer effectively.
- [fact; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7645890/] The same study concludes that quantitative measures alone are unlikely to capture all factors contributing to tacit-knowledge transfer, because cultural implications, employee relationships, and other contextual factors affect outcomes.
- [fact; source: https://people.bu.edu/carlile/docs/Pragmatic%20View%20of%20Knowledge%20%28Carlile%29.pdf] Carlile describes knowledge in practice as localized, embedded, and invested within a function, and argues that these characteristics generate knowledge boundaries across specialised groups.
- [inference; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7645890/; https://people.bu.edu/carlile/docs/Pragmatic%20View%20of%20Knowledge%20%28Carlile%29.pdf] Verification cost rises sharply when the seeker must judge not just whether a document is complete, but whether the tacit, local, and relational preconditions of correct use are present.

#### E. Mechanisms that reduce abandonment risk

- [fact; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.2016.1049] Szulanski, Ringov, and Jensen find that front-loading tacit knowledge exchange reduces transfer difficulty when causal ambiguity is high, but increases difficulty when the relationship between source and recipient is arduous.
- [fact; source: https://people.bu.edu/carlile/docs/Pragmatic%20View%20of%20Knowledge%20%28Carlile%29.pdf; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1177%2F030631289019003001] Carlile and Star and Griesemer both describe boundary objects as representations or standardized forms that are adaptable across viewpoints while robust enough to maintain a common identity.
- [fact; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1108%2F13673270210417664] Goh argues that effective knowledge transfer depends on a balanced organisational approach that addresses the sender, recipient, context, content, and transfer mechanisms rather than treating transfer as a purely technical publishing problem.
- [inference; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.2016.1049; https://people.bu.edu/carlile/docs/Pragmatic%20View%20of%20Knowledge%20%28Carlile%29.pdf; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1108%2F13673270210417664] The strongest transfer designs therefore do not try to eliminate specialist depth; they stage it by front-loading tacit exchange where ambiguity is high, using boundary objects to create a shared referent, and raising recipient capability before expecting independent reuse.

#### Prior completed-item continuity

- [fact; source: https://github.com/davidamitchell/Research/blob/main/Research/completed/2026-05-09-key-person-dependency-basel-risk-linkage.md] The key-person dependency item shows that organisations often discover tacit-knowledge concentration only when an operational-risk or continuity surface is already exposed.
- [fact; source: https://github.com/davidamitchell/Research/blob/main/Research/completed/2026-05-09-prc-risk-scoring-unstandardized-workforce-processes.md] The risk-scoring item shows that tacit knowledge and undocumented local workarounds can stay invisible to formal control systems even when the surrounding process looks standardised.
- [inference; source: https://github.com/davidamitchell/Research/blob/main/Research/completed/2026-05-09-key-person-dependency-basel-risk-linkage.md; https://github.com/davidamitchell/Research/blob/main/Research/completed/2026-05-09-prc-risk-scoring-unstandardized-workforce-processes.md] Those governance findings are consistent with the transfer mechanism here: when knowledge remains highly specific and hard for non-specialists to verify, organisations often substitute dependence, escalation, or local workaround tolerance for genuine transfer.

### §3 Reasoning

- [inference; source: https://www.nber.org/system/files/chapters/c2144/c2144.pdf; https://eric.ed.gov/?id=EJ406851] The mechanism is cumulative rather than single-cause: asset specificity creates a verification problem, the verification problem interacts with low prior knowledge, and the resulting learning burden pushes the seeker toward abandonment.
- [inference; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105; https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/journal-of-management-and-organization/article/abs/determinants-of-causal-ambiguity-and-difficulty-of-knowledge-transfer-within-the-firm/C55CBA4022809A31A9F9D445591A7C67] The strongest empirical support is for the claim that causal ambiguity and low absorptive capacity are proximate transfer barriers, while asset specificity and information asymmetry explain why those barriers remain expensive to resolve.
- [inference; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.2016.1049; https://people.bu.edu/carlile/docs/Pragmatic%20View%20of%20Knowledge%20%28Carlile%29.pdf; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1108%2F13673270210417664] Transfer mechanisms work when they reduce ambiguity or build recipient capability, not when they merely add more documentation to a context the seeker still cannot interpret.

### §4 Consistency Check

- [inference; source: https://www.nber.org/system/files/chapters/c2144/c2144.pdf; https://eric.ed.gov/?id=EJ406851; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105] The evidence is internally consistent: Arrow explains why expert knowledge is hard to price and verify before disclosure, Cohen and Levinthal explain why some recipients cannot process what they receive, and Szulanski shows that lack of absorptive capacity and causal ambiguity do in fact predict transfer difficulty.
- [inference; source: https://people.bu.edu/carlile/docs/Pragmatic%20View%20of%20Knowledge%20%28Carlile%29.pdf; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1177%2F030631289019003001; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7645890/] The mechanism-level sources also agree that localized knowledge requires translation objects, social interaction, or contextual interpretation rather than pure codification.
- [inference; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.2016.1049] The main boundary condition is relational: front-loading tacit exchange helps when ambiguity is high, but not when the source-recipient relationship is already arduous.

### §5 Depth and Breadth Expansion

- [inference; source: https://www.nber.org/system/files/chapters/c2144/c2144.pdf; https://github.com/davidamitchell/Research/blob/main/Research/completed/2026-03-10-nature-of-the-firm-coase-organisations.md] Economic lens: highly specific expert knowledge behaves less like a commodity and more like a governance problem, because ex ante pricing and ex post redeployment are both weak.
- [inference; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7645890/; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1108%2F13673270210417664] Behavioural lens: seekers do not merely fail cognitively; they also respond rationally to high learning burden by deferring, narrowing scope, or relying on proxies such as credentials and templates.
- [inference; source: https://people.bu.edu/carlile/docs/Pragmatic%20View%20of%20Knowledge%20%28Carlile%29.pdf; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1177%2F030631289019003001] Organisational-design lens: boundary objects, standardized forms, and shared representations lower coordination cost, but they do not dissolve specialist boundaries unless the recipient can connect the representation to local action.
- [inference; source: https://github.com/davidamitchell/Research/blob/main/Research/completed/2026-05-09-key-person-dependency-basel-risk-linkage.md; https://github.com/davidamitchell/Research/blob/main/Research/completed/2026-05-09-prc-risk-scoring-unstandardized-workforce-processes.md] Governance lens: unresolved transfer failure often reappears later as key-person dependency, undocumented workarounds, and weak control visibility.

### §6 Synthesis

**Executive summary**

Highly specific expert knowledge blocks transfer when seekers cannot value it before disclosure, cannot absorb it without prior related knowledge, and cannot cheaply verify that they have understood it correctly. [inference; source: https://www.nber.org/system/files/chapters/c2144/c2144.pdf; https://eric.ed.gov/?id=EJ406851; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105]
The best-supported proximate barriers are low absorptive capacity, the recipient's ability to recognize, assimilate, and apply external knowledge, causal ambiguity, uncertainty about which elements make the knowledge work, and weak source-recipient relationships, not simple unwillingness to share. [inference; source: https://eric.ed.gov/?id=EJ406851; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105; https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/journal-of-management-and-organization/article/abs/determinants-of-causal-ambiguity-and-difficulty-of-knowledge-transfer-within-the-firm/C55CBA4022809A31A9F9D445591A7C67]
Tacit, localized, and context-bound knowledge raises verification cost because documentation alone does not reveal the conditions of correct use. [inference; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7645890/; https://people.bu.edu/carlile/docs/Pragmatic%20View%20of%20Knowledge%20%28Carlile%29.pdf]
Transfer improves when organisations stage tacit exchange, use boundary objects, shared forms or representations that preserve a common reference across groups, or use standardized forms to create a shared reference, and deliberately build recipient capability before expecting independent reuse. [inference; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.2016.1049; https://people.bu.edu/carlile/docs/Pragmatic%20View%20of%20Knowledge%20%28Carlile%29.pdf; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1177%2F030631289019003001; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1108%2F13673270210417664]

**Key findings**

1. **Highly specific expert knowledge creates a valuation problem because seekers often need partial disclosure or expert mediation to judge its worth, yet that same disclosure starts transferring the knowledge before a price or commitment is settled.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://www.nber.org/system/files/chapters/c2144/c2144.pdf; https://github.com/davidamitchell/Research/blob/main/Research/completed/2026-03-10-nature-of-the-firm-coase-organisations.md)
2. **Absorptive capacity is a binding constraint on knowledge transfer because recipients need prior related knowledge to recognize, assimilate, and apply external expertise rather than merely receiving a larger volume of information.** ([fact]; high confidence; source: https://eric.ed.gov/?id=EJ406851; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105)
3. **Empirical stickiness research shows that difficult transfers are driven mainly by recipient-side absorptive limits, causal ambiguity, and arduous source-recipient relationships, which supports the inference that some seekers will defer or abandon the pathway when the transfer burden stays high and benefits remain uncertain.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.2016.1049)
4. **Causal ambiguity rises when knowledge is complex, tacit, local, and weakly connected to the recipient's existing knowledge base, making it hard for seekers to know which elements matter and whether apparent understanding is real.** ([fact]; high confidence; source: https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/journal-of-management-and-organization/article/abs/determinants-of-causal-ambiguity-and-difficulty-of-knowledge-transfer-within-the-firm/C55CBA4022809A31A9F9D445591A7C67; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105)
5. **Tacit knowledge remains difficult to verify and transfer through documents or quantitative measurement alone because effective use depends on context, relationships, and situated performance rather than on codified content by itself.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7645890/; https://people.bu.edu/carlile/docs/Pragmatic%20View%20of%20Knowledge%20%28Carlile%29.pdf)
6. **Boundary objects, standardized forms, and other translational artifacts help because they preserve a common reference across specialist groups, but they reduce abandonment risk only when paired with enough interaction or capability-building for seekers to interpret them locally.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://people.bu.edu/carlile/docs/Pragmatic%20View%20of%20Knowledge%20%28Carlile%29.pdf; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1177%2F030631289019003001; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1108%2F13673270210417664)
7. **When causal ambiguity is high, front-loading tacit exchange can reduce transfer difficulty, but the same tactic backfires under an arduous relationship, so trust and working relationship quality are not optional complements to transfer design.** ([fact]; medium confidence; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.2016.1049)
8. **If organisations do not solve this transfer problem, they often surface it later as key-person dependency, undocumented workarounds, and weak governance visibility rather than as a formally recognized knowledge-transfer failure.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://github.com/davidamitchell/Research/blob/main/Research/completed/2026-05-09-key-person-dependency-basel-risk-linkage.md; https://github.com/davidamitchell/Research/blob/main/Research/completed/2026-05-09-prc-risk-scoring-unstandardized-workforce-processes.md)

## Findings

### Executive Summary

Highly specific expert knowledge blocks transfer when seekers cannot value it before disclosure, cannot absorb it without prior related knowledge, and cannot cheaply verify that they have understood it correctly. [inference; source: https://www.nber.org/system/files/chapters/c2144/c2144.pdf; https://eric.ed.gov/?id=EJ406851; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105]
The best-supported proximate barriers are low absorptive capacity, the recipient's ability to recognize, assimilate, and apply external knowledge, causal ambiguity, uncertainty about which elements make the knowledge work, and weak source-recipient relationships, not simple unwillingness to share. [inference; source: https://eric.ed.gov/?id=EJ406851; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105; https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/journal-of-management-and-organization/article/abs/determinants-of-causal-ambiguity-and-difficulty-of-knowledge-transfer-within-the-firm/C55CBA4022809A31A9F9D445591A7C67]
Tacit, localized, and context-bound knowledge raises verification cost because documentation alone does not reveal the conditions of correct use. [inference; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7645890/; https://people.bu.edu/carlile/docs/Pragmatic%20View%20of%20Knowledge%20%28Carlile%29.pdf]
Transfer improves when organisations stage tacit exchange, use boundary objects, shared forms or representations that preserve a common reference across groups, or use standardized forms to create a shared reference, and deliberately build recipient capability before expecting independent reuse. [inference; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.2016.1049; https://people.bu.edu/carlile/docs/Pragmatic%20View%20of%20Knowledge%20%28Carlile%29.pdf; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1177%2F030631289019003001; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1108%2F13673270210417664]

### Key Findings

1. **Highly specific expert knowledge creates a valuation problem because seekers often need partial disclosure or expert mediation to judge its worth, yet that same disclosure starts transferring the knowledge before a price or commitment is settled.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://www.nber.org/system/files/chapters/c2144/c2144.pdf; https://github.com/davidamitchell/Research/blob/main/Research/completed/2026-03-10-nature-of-the-firm-coase-organisations.md)
2. **Absorptive capacity is a binding constraint on knowledge transfer because recipients need prior related knowledge to recognize, assimilate, and apply external expertise rather than merely receiving a larger volume of information.** ([fact]; high confidence; source: https://eric.ed.gov/?id=EJ406851; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105)
3. **Empirical stickiness research shows that difficult transfers are driven mainly by recipient-side absorptive limits, causal ambiguity, and arduous source-recipient relationships, which supports the inference that some seekers will defer or abandon the pathway when the transfer burden stays high and benefits remain uncertain.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.2016.1049)
4. **Causal ambiguity rises when knowledge is complex, tacit, local, and weakly connected to the recipient's existing knowledge base, making it hard for seekers to know which elements matter and whether apparent understanding is real.** ([fact]; high confidence; source: https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/journal-of-management-and-organization/article/abs/determinants-of-causal-ambiguity-and-difficulty-of-knowledge-transfer-within-the-firm/C55CBA4022809A31A9F9D445591A7C67; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105)
5. **Tacit knowledge remains difficult to verify and transfer through documents or quantitative measurement alone because effective use depends on context, relationships, and situated performance rather than on codified content by itself.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7645890/; https://people.bu.edu/carlile/docs/Pragmatic%20View%20of%20Knowledge%20%28Carlile%29.pdf)
6. **Boundary objects, standardized forms, and other translational artifacts help because they preserve a common reference across specialist groups, but they reduce abandonment risk only when paired with enough interaction or capability-building for seekers to interpret them locally.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://people.bu.edu/carlile/docs/Pragmatic%20View%20of%20Knowledge%20%28Carlile%29.pdf; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1177%2F030631289019003001; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1108%2F13673270210417664)
7. **When causal ambiguity is high, front-loading tacit exchange can reduce transfer difficulty, but the same tactic backfires under an arduous relationship, so trust and working relationship quality are not optional complements to transfer design.** ([fact]; medium confidence; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.2016.1049)
8. **If organisations do not solve this transfer problem, they often surface it later as key-person dependency, undocumented workarounds, and weak governance visibility rather than as a formally recognized knowledge-transfer failure.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://github.com/davidamitchell/Research/blob/main/Research/completed/2026-05-09-key-person-dependency-basel-risk-linkage.md; https://github.com/davidamitchell/Research/blob/main/Research/completed/2026-05-09-prc-risk-scoring-unstandardized-workforce-processes.md)
