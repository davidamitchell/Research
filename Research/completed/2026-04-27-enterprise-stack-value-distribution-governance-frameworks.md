## Research Question

## Scope

**In scope:**
- The seven-layer enterprise stack described in the seeded item: data origination, storage, compute, semantic layer, reasoning, action and orchestration, and governance, including the vendor examples associated with each layer in the item setup

#### B. Enterprise-architecture and strategy frameworks

- [fact; source: https://www.opengroup.org/togaf; https://www.opengroup.org/archimate-forum/archimate-overview] TOGAF presents enterprise architecture as a method for aligning business, application, and technology domains, while ArchiMate defines a modeling language for describing and analyzing relationships across those domains.

- [fact; source: https://geoffreyamoore.com/topics/; https://stvp.stanford.edu/av/core-and-context] Geoffrey Moore defines core as what directly contributes to competitive differentiation and customer preference, while context is the necessary but non-differentiating remainder of the business.
- [inference; source: https://geoffreyamoore.com/topics/; https://stvp.stanford.edu/av/core-and-context] Moore's framework predicts that once raw model capability and basic compute cease to differentiate, enduring value migrates toward the layer that still contains unique enterprise context, operating rules, and switching costs.
- [fact; source: https://www.wardleymaps.com/guides/wardley-mapping-101; https://www.wardleymaps.com/book-passages/wardley-on-evolution] Wardley Mapping treats value chains as multi-component systems whose components move from genesis to custom-built to product to commodity stages.

- [fact; source: https://stratechery.com/aggregation-theory/] Aggregation Theory argues that zero distribution costs, zero marginal costs, and zero transaction costs reshape where moats and value accrue in internet-era businesses.
- [inference; source: https://stratechery.com/aggregation-theory] Aggregation Theory is directionally useful here because it highlights how commodity-like lower layers can increase the relative value of controlling customer access, coordination, and rule-setting surfaces, but it was built for internet aggregators rather than enterprise control planes and therefore transfers only partially.
