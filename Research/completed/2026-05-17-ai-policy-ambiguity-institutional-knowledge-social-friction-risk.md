# LLM-First Policy Clarification and Institutional Knowledge Atrophy: Loss of Peer Consultation, Mentoring, and Long-Term Policy Expertise

## Research Question

How does shifting from peer policy clarification to Large Language Model (LLM)-first interaction affect institutional memory transfer, mentoring, and long-term policy expertise?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Measure changes in peer-to-peer consultation frequency after AI-first policy-assistance adoption.
- Assess whether reduced social friction increases low-effort AI reliance for routine interpretation questions.
- Evaluate long-run effects on mentoring pathways and collaborative problem-solving capacity.

**Out of scope:**
- Implementing production policy-assistant software.
- Entity-specific legal advice.
- Exhaustive jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction legal analysis.

**Constraints:** Use publicly available sources, distinguish demonstrated evidence from inference, and prioritise sources with explicit governance or empirical grounding.

#### A. Evidence that AI changes where workers go for expertise

- [fact; source: https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/working-papers/generative-ai-work] Brynjolfsson et al. report that access to a generative AI conversational assistant increased customer-support productivity by 14% on average and by 34% for novice and lower-skilled workers, with suggestive evidence that the system disseminated the best practices of more able workers and helped newer workers move down the experience curve.
- [fact; source: https://reports.weforum.org/docs/WEF_Future_of_Jobs_Report_2025.pdf] The World Economic Forum reports that workplace studies have found generative AI can substitute for tacit knowledge among newer or average-performing workers.
- [fact; source: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1390182/full] Vowels et al. review the growing organisational use of AI advisors in communication, analytics, customer service, finance, medicine, and other decision settings, and argue that AI advice changes how evidence is gathered and used in organisations.
- [inference; source: https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/working-papers/generative-ai-work; https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1390182/full] If an AI assistant can deliver codified best-practice fragments immediately at the point of need, routine questions that would previously have gone to a colleague become more likely to terminate inside the tool rather than inside a peer interaction.

#### B. Evidence that low-friction AI help can weaken long-run skill formation

- [fact; source: https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20245v1] Shen and Tamkin report randomized experiments in software engineering showing that AI assistance on tasks involving a new asynchronous Python library reduced evaluation scores on conceptual understanding, code reading, and debugging by 17% overall, while producing no statistically significant average speed improvement.
- [fact; source: https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20245v1] The same study found that participants who fully delegated coding tasks to the AI assistant showed some local productivity improvements but learned less about the unfamiliar library, while cognitively engaged interaction patterns preserved more learning.
- [fact; source: https://par.nsf.gov/biblio/10545734-does-using-artificial-intelligence-assistance-accelerate-skill-decay-hinder-skill-development-without-performers-awareness] Macnamara et al. argue that AI assistants can accelerate skill decay among experts, hinder skill acquisition among learners, and obscure those losses from the users themselves.
- [fact; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-08-ai-skill-decay-deskilling-measurement-interventions.html] The repository's prior skill-decay synthesis already found that the most vulnerable capabilities under automation pressure are verification, debugging, anomaly detection, and situational awareness rather than every execution skill equally.
- [inference; source: https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20245v1; https://par.nsf.gov/biblio/10545734-does-using-artificial-intelligence-assistance-accelerate-skill-decay-hinder-skill-development-without-performers-awareness; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-08-ai-skill-decay-deskilling-measurement-interventions.html] AI-first policy clarification risks reducing policy expertise because fewer workers repeatedly practice interpretation, challenge, and exception-handling unaided.

#### C. Why peer consultation matters for mentoring and institutional memory transfer

- [fact; source: https://www.aft.org/ae/winter1991/collins_brown_holum] Collins, Brown, and Holum describe cognitive apprenticeship as a learning model built on modeling, scaffolding, fading, coaching, observation of multiple experts, and social participation in a community of practice.
- [fact; source: https://www.aft.org/ae/winter1991/collins_brown_holum] The same article argues that apprenticeship works partly because learners observe how experts think, not just what answer they produce, and because they see multiple ways to perform the task rather than one frozen procedure.
- [fact; source: https://www.nationalacademies.org/read/27644/chapter/2] The National Academies state that realising AI benefits requires complementary investments in new skills and new organisational processes and structures, and that AI adoption changes which forms of expertise are demanded and how work is organised.
- [fact; source: https://reports.weforum.org/docs/WEF_Future_of_Jobs_Report_2025.pdf] The World Economic Forum reports that analytical thinking remains the most sought-after core skill among employers in 2025, followed by resilience, flexibility and agility, and leadership and social influence.
- [inference; source: https://www.aft.org/ae/winter1991/collins_brown_holum; https://www.nationalacademies.org/read/27644/chapter/2; https://reports.weforum.org/docs/WEF_Future_of_Jobs_Report_2025.pdf] When routine clarification moves from peers to an AI assistant, organisations do not only change answer speed; they also reduce occasions where juniors watch expert reasoning, ask follow-up questions, and learn the contextual judgment that feeds institutional memory.

#### D. Evidence and counter-evidence on AI substituting for some mentoring functions

- [fact; source: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1364054/full] Barger et al. found that participants in a randomized coaching experiment formed similarly moderately high working alliances with a simulated AI coach and a human coach during one guided 60-minute session.
- [fact; source: https://researchonline.lse.ac.uk/id/eprint/123856/1/Confirmation_bias_in_AI-assisted_decision-making.pdf; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10772030/] Bashkirova and Krpan found that AI recommendations were trusted more when they matched the decision-maker's preliminary view, and Vicente and Matute found that incorrect AI support reduced human accuracy more strongly when it appeared before people formed their own judgment.
- [fact; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-17-ai-policy-ambiguity-accountability-governance-risk.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-17-ai-policy-ambiguity-cognitive-closure-confirmation-bias-risk.html] Adjacent completed items in this repository already show that policy-assistant use can suppress escalation and reward fast closure unless the workflow forces independent reasoning and override authority.
- [inference; source: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1364054/full; https://researchonline.lse.ac.uk/id/eprint/123856/1/Confirmation_bias_in_AI-assisted_decision-making.pdf; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10772030/] A narrower rival interpretation fits the evidence. AI can substitute for some low-stakes guidance and can feel relationship-like in bounded sessions, but AI-first consultation still changes the learning path because it removes many of the social and interpretive frictions that previously triggered peer explanation, challenge, and escalation.

#### E. Operating-model implications

- [fact; source: https://www.nationalacademies.org/read/27644/chapter/2] The National Academies state that society has a choice in whether AI is used mainly to augment human expertise or to substitute for it.
- [fact; source: https://reports.weforum.org/docs/WEF_Future_of_Jobs_Report_2025.pdf] The World Economic Forum reports that 50% of the workforce has completed training, reskilling, or upskilling measures, and that employers continue to rank analytical and social influence skills among the most important core capabilities.
- [inference; source: https://www.nationalacademies.org/read/27644/chapter/2; https://reports.weforum.org/docs/WEF_Future_of_Jobs_Report_2025.pdf; https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20245v1; https://www.aft.org/ae/winter1991/collins_brown_holum] The evidence supports keeping the tool in an augmentation role while preserving structured colleague escalation, apprenticeship-style explanation, and periodic unaided interpretation practice for ambiguous or high-consequence policy questions.

### §3 Reasoning

- [fact; source: https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/working-papers/generative-ai-work; https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20245v1] The direct empirical evidence supports two claims simultaneously: generative AI can diffuse codified expert practice to less experienced workers, and heavy reliance on it during unfamiliar tasks can weaken the learning of underlying concepts.
- [fact; source: https://www.aft.org/ae/winter1991/collins_brown_holum] The mentoring literature consulted here supports the narrower claim that expertise transfer involves visible reasoning, coaching, staged responsibility, and access to multiple models of expert judgment, not only answer delivery.
- [inference; source: https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/working-papers/generative-ai-work; https://www.aft.org/ae/winter1991/collins_brown_holum; https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20245v1] The central synthesis is that AI-first clarification selectively replaces the part of consultation that is fastest to codify while shrinking the social pathway through which contextual interpretation skills are learned and refreshed.
- [assumption; source: https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/working-papers/generative-ai-work; https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1390182/full] Working assumption: routine internal policy questions share enough structure with other workplace advice-seeking tasks that substitution effects on help-seeking location are directionally informative even though the consulted studies do not count policy-specific consultation events.

### §4 Consistency Check

- [fact; source: https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/working-papers/generative-ai-work; https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20245v1] The main tension in the evidence is that AI can improve short-run performance for novices while still impairing deeper learning on unfamiliar tasks.
- [fact; source: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1364054/full; https://www.aft.org/ae/winter1991/collins_brown_holum] A second tension is that AI can simulate some one-session guidance functions while the apprenticeship source still treats expertise formation as deeply social and observational.
- [inference; source: https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/working-papers/generative-ai-work; https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20245v1; https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1364054/full; https://www.aft.org/ae/winter1991/collins_brown_holum] These tensions resolve when the workflow is separated into two layers. AI can plausibly handle quick codified support and some basic coaching interactions, while human consultation stays important for contextual judgment formation, exception handling, and the transfer of tacit organisational reasoning.
- [inference; source: https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/working-papers/generative-ai-work; https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20245v1; https://www.aft.org/ae/winter1991/collins_brown_holum] Overall confidence stays medium because the direction of risk is well-supported, but there is no consulted field study that directly measures policy-consultation frequency before and after AI-first clarification adoption.

### §5 Depth and Breadth Expansion

**Technical lens**

- [fact; source: https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20245v1] The most exposed technical capabilities are supervision skills, especially conceptual understanding, code reading, debugging, and error correction under unfamiliar conditions.

**Behavioural lens**

- [fact; source: https://researchonline.lse.ac.uk/id/eprint/123856/1/Confirmation_bias_in_AI-assisted_decision-making.pdf; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10772030/] Congruent or early-presented AI advice changes user acceptance patterns, which means low-friction tool access is not neutral when ambiguity is high.

**Organisational lens**

- [fact; source: https://www.nationalacademies.org/read/27644/chapter/2; https://reports.weforum.org/docs/WEF_Future_of_Jobs_Report_2025.pdf] Workforce evidence from the National Academies and the World Economic Forum frames the adjustment challenge as organisational, not purely technical, because firms need new skills, new processes, and explicit investment in expertise.

**Historical lens**

- [fact; source: https://www.nationalacademies.org/read/27644/chapter/2] The National Academies place AI in the longer history of general-purpose technologies that strand some existing expertise while creating demand for new forms of expertise.

**Governance lens**

- [inference; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-17-ai-policy-ambiguity-accountability-governance-risk.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-17-ai-policy-ambiguity-authority-drift-policy-decay-risk.html; https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20245v1] The governance consequence is that expertise erosion and escalation suppression can reinforce each other: fewer peer interactions reduce learning opportunities, and weaker learning can make future escalation and challenge less likely.

### §6 Synthesis

**Executive summary:**

Shifting routine policy clarification from colleagues to an AI-first workflow is likely to reduce incidental mentoring and weaken long-run policy expertise unless organisations deliberately preserve human escalation and apprenticeship pathways. [inference; source: https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/working-papers/generative-ai-work; https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20245v1; https://www.aft.org/ae/winter1991/collins_brown_holum] The strongest direct evidence does not show that AI inevitably destroys expertise; instead it shows a mixed pattern in which AI diffuses codified best practices quickly, especially to novices, while heavier dependence during unfamiliar tasks reduces conceptual understanding and debugging ability. [fact; source: https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/working-papers/generative-ai-work; https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20245v1] Apprenticeship evidence suggests that peer consultation matters because it transfers visible reasoning, contextual judgment, and multiple models of expert practice, not only final answers. [inference; source: https://www.aft.org/ae/winter1991/collins_brown_holum] For policy interpretation, the evidence supports augmentation rather than substitution. Use AI to speed low-stakes retrieval and drafting, but preserve colleague-first or mandatory-human escalation for ambiguous, exceptional, or high-consequence questions. [inference; source: https://www.nationalacademies.org/read/27644/chapter/2; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-17-ai-policy-ambiguity-accountability-governance-risk.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-17-ai-policy-ambiguity-authority-drift-policy-decay-risk.html]

**Key findings:**

1. **The evidence suggests that generative AI can displace some routine peer consultation by delivering codified expert practice directly to less experienced workers at the point of need.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/working-papers/generative-ai-work; https://reports.weforum.org/docs/WEF_Future_of_Jobs_Report_2025.pdf)
2. **The same shift can weaken long-run skill formation when workers rely heavily on AI for unfamiliar tasks, because direct software evidence shows lower conceptual understanding, debugging, and independent error-correction after delegation-heavy use.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20245v1; https://par.nsf.gov/biblio/10545734-does-using-artificial-intelligence-assistance-accelerate-skill-decay-hinder-skill-development-without-performers-awareness)
3. **Apprenticeship evidence suggests that peer clarification pathways carry institutional memory partly through modeling, coaching, scaffolding, and exposure to multiple experts, which AI answer delivery does not inherently preserve.** ([inference]; low confidence; source: https://www.aft.org/ae/winter1991/collins_brown_holum)

## Findings

### Executive Summary

Shifting routine policy clarification from colleagues to an AI-first workflow is likely to reduce incidental mentoring and weaken long-run policy expertise unless organisations deliberately preserve human escalation and apprenticeship pathways. [inference; source: https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/working-papers/generative-ai-work; https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20245v1; https://www.aft.org/ae/winter1991/collins_brown_holum] The strongest direct evidence shows a mixed pattern: generative AI can quickly diffuse codified best practices to newer workers, but heavier reliance during unfamiliar tasks reduces conceptual understanding and debugging ability rather than building durable expertise automatically. [fact; source: https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/working-papers/generative-ai-work; https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20245v1] Apprenticeship evidence suggests that peer consultation matters because it transfers visible reasoning, contextual judgment, and multiple models of expert practice, not only final answers. [inference; source: https://www.aft.org/ae/winter1991/collins_brown_holum] For policy interpretation, the evidence supports augmentation rather than substitution. Use AI to accelerate low-stakes retrieval and drafting, but preserve colleague explanation, challenge, and escalation for ambiguous or high-consequence cases. [inference; source: https://www.nationalacademies.org/read/27644/chapter/2; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-17-ai-policy-ambiguity-accountability-governance-risk.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-17-ai-policy-ambiguity-authority-drift-policy-decay-risk.html]

### Key Findings

1. **The evidence suggests that generative AI can displace some routine peer consultation by delivering codified expert practice directly to less experienced workers at the point of need.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/working-papers/generative-ai-work; https://reports.weforum.org/docs/WEF_Future_of_Jobs_Report_2025.pdf)

### Analysis

The evidence does not support a simple pro-AI or anti-AI story. [inference; source: https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/working-papers/generative-ai-work; https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20245v1; https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1364054/full] Field evidence shows that generative AI can spread codified best practices and improve novice performance, which gives organisations a real incentive to keep more routine questions inside the tool. [inference; source: https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/working-papers/generative-ai-work] At the same time, the strongest direct skill-formation study shows that delegation during unfamiliar work lowers conceptual understanding and debugging ability, which means fewer peer interactions are not a free substitute for learning. [fact; source: https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20245v1; https://par.nsf.gov/biblio/10545734-does-using-artificial-intelligence-assistance-accelerate-skill-decay-hinder-skill-development-without-performers-awareness] The decisive mechanism is that peer clarification carries explanation, context, and staged responsibility, while AI-first clarification mainly carries fast codified output. [inference; source: https://www.aft.org/ae/winter1991/collins_brown_holum; https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/working-papers/generative-ai-work] A plausible rival explanation is that organisations could replace lost hallway mentoring with formal training or hybrid coaching, and the bounded coaching evidence suggests some of that substitution is possible. [inference; source: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1364054/full; https://www.nationalacademies.org/read/27644/chapter/2] That rival does not remove the risk, because it requires deliberate organisational design; absent that design, AI-first convenience will tend to remove the social friction that previously triggered explanation, challenge, and escalation. [inference; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10772030/; https://researchonline.lse.ac.uk/id/eprint/123856/1/Confirmation_bias_in_AI-assisted_decision-making.pdf; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-17-ai-policy-ambiguity-cognitive-closure-confirmation-bias-risk.html]

- [fact; source: https://arxiv.org/html/2601.20245v1; https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/working-papers/generative-ai-work] No consulted study directly measures peer-policy-consultation frequency before and after AI-first policy-assistant adoption.
