# How Do Formal Governance Structures Distort Cross-Department Knowledge Flows?

## Research Question

How do formal governance mechanisms such as hierarchy, reporting lines, and mandatory protocols reshape cross-department knowledge flow, and when do they unintentionally raise cross-department transaction costs, the extra search, access, interpretation, and approval costs created by crossing a formal boundary?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Effects of formal governance structures on cross-boundary knowledge transfer.
- Tensions between compliance, control, and adaptive collaboration.
- Conditions under which governance enables versus suppresses useful lateral exchange.

**Out of scope:**
- Legal analysis of national labour law regimes.
- Detailed redesign of one specific company structure.

**Constraints:** Prioritise institutional and organisational-design evidence that links formal rules to measurable collaboration outcomes.

## Context

Formal governance can prevent coordination failure, but it can also redirect effort into approval seeking, expert discovery, and status management instead of knowledge transfer, so the core question is which rule designs reduce uncertainty and which merely relocate it across departmental boundaries. [inference; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171110; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Fmnsc.49.4.432.14428; https://ostromworkshop.indiana.edu/courses-teaching/teaching-tools/ostrom-design/index.html]

## Approach

1. Identify formal governance features that correlate with increased transfer friction.
2. Analyse interaction effects between formal controls and informal coordination norms.
3. Produce design criteria for governance that preserves accountability without persistent blockage of lateral exchange.

#### A. What formal governance does well

- [fact; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171110] Grant conceptualises the firm as an institution for integrating specialist knowledge and argues that organisational design must address hierarchy and the distribution of decision-making authority because coordination, not only knowledge creation, determines performance.
- [fact; source: https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511807763; https://ostromworkshop.indiana.edu/courses-teaching/teaching-tools/ostrom-design/index.html] Ostrom's governance work argues that robust institutions use clear rules, boundaries, monitoring, and locally fitted arrangements rather than relying only on market exchange or blanket external control.
- [inference; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171110; https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511807763; https://ostromworkshop.indiana.edu/courses-teaching/teaching-tools/ostrom-design/index.html] Formal governance lowers coordination cost when it clarifies who can decide, who monitors exceptions, and how participants know the rules before they need cross-department help.

#### B. Where distortion enters

- [fact; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.13.2.179.536] Tsai reports that formal hierarchical structure, in the form of centralization, has a significant negative effect on intraorganizational knowledge sharing in a large multiunit company.
- [fact; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.13.2.179.536] The same study finds that informal lateral relations, in the form of social interaction, have a significant positive effect on knowledge sharing among units that compete with one another for market share.
- [fact; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.2307%2F2667032] Hansen's network study of 120 new-product-development projects across 41 divisions finds that weak interunit ties help teams search for useful knowledge in other subunits but impede the transfer of complex knowledge, which tends to require strong ties.
- [fact; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.2307%2F2667032] Hansen also finds that weak interunit ties speed projects when transferred knowledge is not complex but slow projects when the knowledge to be transferred is highly complex.
- [inference; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.13.2.179.536; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.2307%2F2667032] Centralized governance distorts cross-department flow when it substitutes vertical routing for lateral relationship building, because search may still succeed while complex interpretation and adaptation fail.

#### C. Why transaction costs rise at formal boundaries

- [fact; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Fmnsc.49.4.432.14428] Borgatti and Cross show that the probability of seeking information from another person depends on knowing what that person knows, valuing what that person knows, gaining timely access to that person's thinking, and perceiving that seeking information would not be too costly.
- [fact; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105] Szulanski finds that major barriers to internal best-practice transfer are the recipient's lack of absorptive capacity, causal ambiguity, and an arduous source-recipient relationship rather than motivation alone.
- [fact; source: https://repub.eur.nl/pub/6474] van den Bosch, Volberda, and de Boer argue that absorptive capacity depends not only on prior related knowledge but also on organisational forms and combinative capabilities, the routines that let organisations coordinate and integrate knowledge.
- [fact; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-how-do-activation-energy-barriers-shape-knowledge-seeking-behaviour.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-what-are-the-micro-transaction-costs-of-internal-knowledge-sourcing.html] Adjacent completed items in this repository show that knowledge requests stall when expertise is hard to identify, hard to reach, socially risky to ask for, or too costly to interpret safely after contact.
- [inference; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Fmnsc.49.4.432.14428; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105; https://repub.eur.nl/pub/6474; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-what-are-the-micro-transaction-costs-of-internal-knowledge-sourcing.html] Reporting lines and mandatory approval steps raise cross-department transaction costs when they add delay before contact, require extra translation after contact, or make the seeker rely on authorities who lack the local context needed to resolve ambiguity.

#### D. When protocols become coercive rather than enabling

- [fact; source: http://faculty.marshall.usc.edu/Paul-Adler/research/Ambivalence%20.pdf] Adler describes a research stream in which bureaucracy can appear as a ceremonial mask, a coercive weapon, or an enabling tool, and he argues that employees often experience bureaucracy as simultaneously enabling and coercive.
- [fact; source: http://faculty.marshall.usc.edu/Paul-Adler/research/Ambivalence%20.pdf] Adler's discussion of the Toyota production system example shows that standardised work can support problem solving and knowledge sharing while also intensifying work and reducing discretion, producing ambivalent worker responses.
- [fact; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-16-governance-structures-build-mode-without-full-accountability-colocation.html] The adjacent governance-structures item shows that separated functions need explicit prioritisation, investment, and exception-handling rights; otherwise formal governance leaves trade-offs to informal negotiation.
- [inference; source: http://faculty.marshall.usc.edu/Paul-Adler/research/Ambivalence%20.pdf; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-16-governance-structures-build-mode-without-full-accountability-colocation.html] Protocols become coercive for cross-department knowledge work when they intensify compliance labor without granting the local exception rights, contextual access, or repair channels needed to resolve real work problems.

#### E. Which institutional designs preserve flow without losing accountability

- [fact; source: https://ostromworkshop.indiana.edu/courses-teaching/teaching-tools/ostrom-design/index.html] The Ostrom Workshop summary of robust institutions emphasises clearly defined boundaries, congruence between rules and local conditions, participant involvement in modifying rules, monitoring, graduated sanctions, conflict-resolution mechanisms, and nested enterprises for larger systems.
- [fact; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-trust-institutions-vs-incentive-schemes-knowledge-sharing.html] The adjacent trust-based-institutions item concludes that trusted pathways lower repeated exchange cost because they remove status risk from asking and reduce the need to renegotiate reciprocity each time.
- [fact; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html] The low-effort-pathways item argues that workers stay on the route with the lowest combined search, access, interpretation, and social-risk burden.
- [inference; source: https://ostromworkshop.indiana.edu/courses-teaching/teaching-tools/ostrom-design/index.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-trust-institutions-vs-incentive-schemes-knowledge-sharing.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-16-governance-structures-build-mode-without-full-accountability-colocation.html] Governance preserves cross-department knowledge flow when it centralizes accountability and exception rights but keeps routine discovery, first contact, and low-risk clarification cheap, visible, and socially safe.

### §3 Reasoning

- [fact; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.13.2.179.536; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.2307%2F2667032] Direct evidence in the consulted studies shows that vertical centralization and weak relational ties have different effects on different stages of transfer, with search benefiting from broader reach but complex transfer benefiting from stronger lateral relationships.
- [fact; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Fmnsc.49.4.432.14428; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105; https://repub.eur.nl/pub/6474] The mechanism shown across the consulted studies is multi-part rather than singular, because discovery, access, interpretation, and relationship quality each affect whether cross-boundary transfer will occur and whether it will succeed.
- [inference; source: http://faculty.marshall.usc.edu/Paul-Adler/research/Ambivalence%20.pdf; https://ostromworkshop.indiana.edu/courses-teaching/teaching-tools/ostrom-design/index.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-16-governance-structures-build-mode-without-full-accountability-colocation.html] The synthesis from the consulted evidence is that formal governance reduces ambiguity at the routing and accountability layer but raises cost when it monopolises interpretation and exception handling that require local context or lateral trust.

### §4 Consistency Check

- [fact; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.13.2.179.536] Tsai shows that centralization has a negative effect on knowledge sharing, so any claim that hierarchy is always beneficial would overstate the evidence.
- [fact; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171110; https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511807763] Grant and Ostrom both show that formal structure remains necessary for coordination and robust rule systems, so any claim that rules should simply be removed would also overstate the evidence.
- [inference; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.13.2.179.536; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.2307%2F2667032; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Fmnsc.49.4.432.14428; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105] The apparent contradiction resolves when governance is separated by function: formal routing, boundaries, and exception rights can reduce uncertainty, while mandatory vertical mediation of complex knowledge raises search, translation, and approval cost.
- [inference; source: http://faculty.marshall.usc.edu/Paul-Adler/research/Ambivalence%20.pdf; https://ostromworkshop.indiana.edu/courses-teaching/teaching-tools/ostrom-design/index.html] No unresolved contradiction remains once bureaucracy is treated as potentially enabling or coercive depending on whether rules fit local work, allow repair, and preserve participant agency.

### §5 Depth and Breadth Expansion

**Behavioural lens**

- [fact; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Fmnsc.49.4.432.14428; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-how-do-activation-energy-barriers-shape-knowledge-seeking-behaviour.html] Formal boundaries change behavior before any knowledge transfer starts, because workers must judge who knows what, whether access will be timely, and whether asking across a hierarchy will be socially safe.

**Economic lens**

- [inference; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-what-are-the-micro-transaction-costs-of-internal-knowledge-sourcing.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html] Distortion is best understood as a transaction-cost shift: governance fails when it makes the authoritative route slower or harder than self-solving, local workaround reuse, or unofficial channels.

**Organisational-design lens**

- [fact; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-16-governance-structures-build-mode-without-full-accountability-colocation.html; https://ostromworkshop.indiana.edu/courses-teaching/teaching-tools/ostrom-design/index.html] Accountability and participation are complements rather than substitutes, because robust systems need named decision rights at the top and locally fitted rule adjustment at the edge.

**Knowledge lens**

- [inference; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.2307%2F2667032; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-how-do-asset-specificity-and-information-asymmetry-block-knowledge-transfer.html] The more tacit, context-specific, or causally ambiguous the knowledge, the less effective universal protocols become and the more valuable strong ties, trusted integrators, and shared context objects become.

### §6 Synthesis

#### Executive Summary

Formal governance distorts cross-department knowledge flows when it centralizes routing, approval, or interpretation in ways that increase search, access, and translation cost faster than it reduces uncertainty. [inference; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.13.2.179.536; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Fmnsc.49.4.432.14428; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105]
Direct empirical evidence shows that centralization reduces intraorganizational knowledge sharing, while weak ties help search but fail for complex transfer unless stronger lateral relationships exist. [fact; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.13.2.179.536; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.2307%2F2667032]
Formal governance remains necessary for accountability and rule clarity, but it works best when routine clarification stays cheap and local, while ambiguous or high-impact cases escalate through named exception paths rather than universal synchronous approval. [inference; source: https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511807763; https://ostromworkshop.indiana.edu/courses-teaching/teaching-tools/ostrom-design/index.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-16-governance-structures-build-mode-without-full-accountability-colocation.html]
The practical design implication is to keep hierarchy for ownership and exception handling while separating it from day-to-day knowledge mediation, so governance clarifies ownership without becoming the most expensive part of the transfer path. [inference; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171110; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-what-are-the-micro-transaction-costs-of-internal-knowledge-sourcing.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html]

#### Key Findings

1. **Formal centralization reduces knowledge sharing across organisational units, while informal lateral relations raise it, in Tsai's study of a large multiunit company whose business units also competed for market share.** ([fact]; medium confidence; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.13.2.179.536)
2. **Weak cross-unit ties help people locate useful knowledge across subunits, but the transfer of complex knowledge still depends on stronger ties and deeper working relationships.** ([fact]; high confidence; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.2307%2F2667032; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105)

## Findings

### Executive Summary

Formal governance distorts cross-department knowledge flows when it centralizes routing, approval, or interpretation in ways that increase search, access, and translation cost faster than it reduces uncertainty. [inference; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.13.2.179.536; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Fmnsc.49.4.432.14428; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171105]
Direct empirical evidence shows that centralization reduces intraorganizational knowledge sharing, while weak ties help search but fail for complex transfer unless stronger lateral relationships exist. [fact; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1287%2Forsc.13.2.179.536; https://api.crossref.org/works/10.2307%2F2667032]
Formal governance remains necessary for accountability and rule clarity, but it works best when routine clarification stays cheap and local, while ambiguous or high-impact cases escalate through named exception paths rather than universal synchronous approval. [inference; source: https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511807763; https://ostromworkshop.indiana.edu/courses-teaching/teaching-tools/ostrom-design/index.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-16-governance-structures-build-mode-without-full-accountability-colocation.html]
The practical design implication is to keep hierarchy for ownership and exception handling while separating it from day-to-day knowledge mediation, so governance clarifies ownership without becoming the most expensive part of the transfer path. [inference; source: https://api.crossref.org/works/10.1002%2Fsmj.4250171110; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-what-are-the-micro-transaction-costs-of-internal-knowledge-sourcing.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html]
