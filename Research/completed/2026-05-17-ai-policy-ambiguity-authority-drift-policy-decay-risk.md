# Authority drift and policy decay from repeated Artificial Intelligence (AI) interpretation

## Research Question

How quickly do repeated unverified Large Language Model (LLM) interpretations create de facto policy norms that diverge from executive intent and board-level risk appetite?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Track time-to-drift from formal policy text to operationally adopted AI-mediated interpretation norms
- Identify mechanisms by which unofficial parallel policy frameworks form and persist
- Assess leading indicators that policy-decay risk is becoming organisationally material

**Out of scope:**
- Implementing production policy-assistant software
- Entity-specific legal advice
- Exhaustive jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction legal analysis

**Constraints:** Use publicly available sources, distinguish demonstrated evidence from inference, and prioritise sources with explicit governance or empirical grounding.

## Context

- Focus: one governance-risk boundary from a broader set of Artificial Intelligence (AI) policy questions.
- Relevance: repeated Large Language Model (LLM) interpretations can become operating precedent before formal policy, risk, and assurance functions catch up. ### §1 Question Decomposition

- **Root question:** how fast can repeated unverified LLM interpretations become de facto policy?
- **A. Drift onset**
  - A1. What event marks the start of drift: first use, first reuse, or only formal adoption?
  - A2. What evidence shows that decision-support outputs are reused with less verification over time?
  - A3. What evidence supports or limits any calendar-style time-to-drift estimate?
- **B. Parallel-framework formation**
  - B1. How do local workarounds and routine exceptions become stable unofficial rules?
  - B2. Which governance responsibilities should interrupt that process?
  - B3. What happens when board-level risk appetite is not translated into concrete operational guidance?
- **C. Materiality**
  - C1. Which signals show that interpretation drift has moved beyond isolated mistakes into operating norm formation?
  - C2. Which signals are measurable by management, risk, compliance, or internal audit?
  - C3. Which rival explanations could account for the same symptoms without LLM-specific reuse?

### §2 Investigation

- [fact; source: https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework] National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) states that the Artificial Intelligence Risk Management Framework (AI RMF) is intended to improve how organisations incorporate trustworthiness into the design, development, use, and evaluation of Artificial Intelligence systems.
- [fact; source: https://www.theiia.org/en/content/communications/2020/july/20-july-2020-iia-issues-important-update-to-three-lines-model/] The Institute of Internal Auditors (IIA) says the 2020 Three Lines Model clarifies oversight by the board or governing body, management and operational leaders including risk and compliance, and independent assurance through internal audit.
- [fact; source: https://www.coso.org/guidance-on-ic] Committee of Sponsoring Organizations of the Treadway Commission (COSO) describes internal controls as a way to help an organisation articulate purpose, set objectives and strategy, and monitor whether information and controls remain effective.
- [fact; source: https://ncua.gov/newsroom/press-release/2022/ncua-board-approves-risk-appetite-statement-briefed-central-liquidity-facility-and-cybersecurity] National Credit Union Administration (NCUA) describes a risk appetite statement as leadership guidance on how much risk the organisation is willing to undertake in pursuit of objectives and as a tool for aligning decisions and resource allocation with strategic goals.
- [fact; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10113449/] Kupfer et al. define automation bias in Artificial Intelligence decision support as thoughtless acceptance of system recommendations and report that higher verification intensity is associated with better decision quality.
- [fact; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10983371/] Litchfield's systematic review finds that over-alerting, distrust in the evidence base, and poor specificity make alerts more likely to be ignored.
- [fact; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9748542/] Murad et al. report that physicians with the highest recent alert exposure were much less likely to respond to a noninterruptive alert than physicians with the lowest recent exposure, with an adjusted odds ratio of 0.38 for the highest versus lowest exposure quartile.
- [fact; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3799168/] Browne and Braden define work-arounds as informal temporary practices for handling exceptions to normal workflow and note that successful work-arounds can be adopted as routine practices.
- [fact; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5649044/] Blijleven et al. find that workarounds arise from workflow mismatches and can hide deficiencies, undermine standardization, and reduce the reliability of information or protocols.
- [fact; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7893618/] Mukherji and Zarhani describe drift as a path in which actors move institutions in a different direction by interpreting rules differently rather than by changing the formal rule text.

#### Prior completed-item sweep

- [fact; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-03-12-swat-technique-loop-fresh-context.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-03-14-organisational-intent-formal-specification.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-09-prc-risk-scoring-unstandardized-workforce-processes.html] The closest completed items already establish three adjacent mechanisms: blind acceptance can compound drift across repeated LLM-mediated steps, narrative translation from organisational intent to downstream artefacts leaves room for reinterpretation, and undocumented local process variants weaken control evidence.

#### A. How drift starts

- [inference; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10113449/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3799168/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7893618/] Drift starts at first reuse rather than at first isolated model output, because a single answer becomes a norm only when someone treats it as a reusable shortcut for later cases, which matches the transition from individual workaround to repeated routine and from rule text to rule reinterpretation.
- [inference; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10113449/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9748542/] Repeated decision-support use can reduce verification pressure before any formal policy change occurs, because automation bias lowers scrutiny and high cumulative alert exposure measurably reduces response rates.
- [fact; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10113449/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9748542/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5649044/] The retrieved public evidence does not provide a universal calendar estimate for time-to-drift across organisations.
- [inference; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10113449/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3799168/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5649044/] The defensible timing claim is therefore process-based: de facto policy can begin to form as soon as staff reuse an unverified LLM interpretation as precedent, and the risk grows with repetition rather than with a fixed elapsed time.

#### B. How unofficial parallel policy frameworks form and persist

- [inference; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3799168/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5649044/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7893618/] Unofficial parallel policy frameworks form when local exception handling becomes the fastest path through an ambiguous or friction-heavy control environment, because repeated work-arounds can harden into routine practice while actors keep the formal policy text unchanged.
- [fact; source: https://www.theiia.org/en/content/communications/2020/july/20-july-2020-iia-issues-important-update-to-three-lines-model/; https://www.coso.org/guidance-on-ic; https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework] The official governance frameworks retrieved here all assume explicit translation, monitoring, and assurance loops between policy intent, operating practice, and review.
- [inference; source: https://www.theiia.org/en/content/communications/2020/july/20-july-2020-iia-issues-important-update-to-three-lines-model/; https://ncua.gov/newsroom/press-release/2022/ncua-board-approves-risk-appetite-statement-briefed-central-liquidity-facility-and-cybersecurity; https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework] Authority drift follows when those loops are weak, because frontline users can start treating model outputs as applied policy while second-line challenge and third-line assurance see only the downstream behaviour or only periodic summaries.
- [inference; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-03-14-organisational-intent-formal-specification.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-09-prc-risk-scoring-unstandardized-workforce-processes.html] Prior repository work sharpens this point: when intent travels through narrative handoffs instead of machine-checkable or standardised controls, local interpretation is easier to normalise and harder to audit.

#### C. Leading indicators that decay is becoming material

- [inference; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9748542/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10983371/] Rising cumulative exposure to policy-assistant prompts or alerts is a leading indicator, because the alert-fatigue evidence shows that heavy recent exposure is associated with lower response and lower attention.
- [inference; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10113449/] Falling verification intensity around model-assisted interpretations is a leading indicator, because automation-bias reduction in the experiment depended on prompting users to check for errors rather than to accept the recommendation flow.
- [inference; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3799168/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5649044/] Growth in unofficial prompt libraries, copied answer fragments, side logs, or exception notes outside the formal policy repository is a leading indicator, because that is how temporary work-arounds become routinized local practice.
- [inference; source: https://www.theiia.org/en/content/communications/2020/july/20-july-2020-iia-issues-important-update-to-three-lines-model/; https://ncua.gov/newsroom/press-release/2022/ncua-board-approves-risk-appetite-statement-briefed-central-liquidity-facility-and-cybersecurity] Widening variance between board-approved risk appetite language and frontline model-assisted decisions is a leading indicator, because risk appetite is supposed to guide managers and staff rather than remain an abstract board artefact.
- [inference; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-03-12-swat-technique-loop-fresh-context.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-09-prc-risk-scoring-unstandardized-workforce-processes.html] Higher concentration of interpretation authority in a small number of tool owners or local experts is a leading indicator, because blind-acceptance loops and undocumented process ownership both make challenge and evidence weaker.

#### D. Rival explanations and limits

- [inference; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5649044/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3799168/] A rival explanation is that ordinary process friction, not LLM usage, creates most local drift; the workaround literature supports that possibility and means this item should not claim that LLMs are the sole cause.
- [inference; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10113449/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9748542/] The best-supported qualification is that LLMs increase the speed and scale of a familiar organisational failure mode by making reusable interpretations cheap, fast, and easy to copy, not by inventing drift from nothing.

### §3 Reasoning

- [fact; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10113449/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9748542/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5649044/] The evidence base supports mechanism claims about automation bias, alert fatigue, work-arounds, and rule reinterpretation.
- [inference; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10113449/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3799168/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7893618/] The answer to "how quickly" must therefore be stated as a mechanism threshold, first reuse and growing repetition, rather than as a universal numerical duration.
- [inference; source: https://www.theiia.org/en/content/communications/2020/july/20-july-2020-iia-issues-important-update-to-three-lines-model/; https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework; https://ncua.gov/newsroom/press-release/2022/ncua-board-approves-risk-appetite-statement-briefed-central-liquidity-facility-and-cybersecurity] Divergence from executive intent becomes governance-significant when translation, monitoring, and assurance are too weak to detect that local precedent has replaced formal policy.
- [assumption; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10113449/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9748542/] This item assumes that decision-support behaviour observed in personnel and healthcare settings transfers directionally to policy-assistant use in enterprises, because no directly matched public study on LLM policy interpretation was located. Justification: the retrieved studies examine repeated recommendation-following and declining attention under exposure, which are the relevant behavioural mechanisms here.

### §5 Depth and Breadth Expansion

- [inference; source: https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework; https://www.coso.org/guidance-on-ic] Technical lens: the more an organisation embeds LLM interpretation into routine policy operations, the more it needs logs, review points, and monitoring artefacts that treat interpretation as a control activity rather than as ad hoc advice.
- [inference; source: https://www.theiia.org/en/content/communications/2020/july/20-july-2020-iia-issues-important-update-to-three-lines-model/; https://ncua.gov/newsroom/press-release/2022/ncua-board-approves-risk-appetite-statement-briefed-central-liquidity-facility-and-cybersecurity] Governance lens: authority drift is not only a frontline behaviour problem; it is also a design failure in how board risk appetite, management guidance, and assurance responsibilities are translated.
- [inference; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10983371/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9748542/] Behavioural lens: fatigue matters because people rarely abandon controls in one dramatic step; they stop challenging low-friction recommendations once exposure is high and perceived marginal value is low.
- [inference; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-04-27-governance-moat-prior-research-implications.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-12-iso-iec-42001-aims-controls-adoption-history.html] Organisational lens: adjacent repository work on governance as durable value and on certifiable AI management systems implies that interpretation governance should be treated as infrastructure, not as optional user hygiene.

### §6 Synthesis

**Executive summary:**

Repeated unverified LLM interpretations can start creating de facto policy norms on the first reused output, but the retrieved public evidence does not support a single universal time-to-drift metric across organisations. [inference; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10113449/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3799168/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7893618/] The evidence describes a mechanism of drift formation: automation bias lowers verification, alert overload lowers responsiveness, and work-arounds let local exceptions harden into routine practice while formal policy text stays unchanged. [inference; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10113449/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10983371/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9748542/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3799168/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5649044/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7893618/] Divergence from executive intent and board-level risk appetite becomes organisationally material when repeated interpretations stop functioning as advice and start functioning as operating precedent without effective oversight, monitoring, and assurance. [inference; source: https://www.coso.org/guidance-on-ic; https://www.theiia.org/en/content/communications/2020/july/20-july-2020-iia-issues-important-update-to-three-lines-model/; https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework; https://ncua.gov/newsroom/press-release/2022/ncua-board-approves-risk-appetite-statement-briefed-central-liquidity-facility-and-cybersecurity] The best-supported early indicators are rising interpretation exposure, falling verification intensity, and growth in unofficial prompt or exception libraries outside the formal policy repository. [inference; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10113449/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9748542/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5649044/]

**Key findings:**

1. **Public evidence supports a threshold view of drift rather than a calendar view, because de facto policy starts when an unverified LLM interpretation is reused as precedent, not only when management formally adopts it or edits the source policy text.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10113449/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3799168/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7893618/)
2. **The retrieved automation-bias evidence implies that repeated model-assisted policy interpretation is risky when users stop verifying recommendations, but that conclusion is an inferential transfer from a single personnel-selection study rather than a direct enterprise policy study.** ([inference]; low confidence; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10113449/)
3. **Alert and prompt overload appear to accelerate authority drift because cumulative exposure reduces responsiveness, which means staff may become less likely to stop, challenge, or independently reason through a model-assisted policy interpretation once the surrounding signal volume becomes routine.** ([inference]; low confidence; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10983371/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9748542/)
4. **Unofficial parallel policy frameworks persist when local work-arounds become routine practices, because repeated exception handling can hide deficiencies, undermine standardization, and move day-to-day behaviour away from the formal rule without any visible policy rewrite.** ([inference]; high confidence; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3799168/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5649044/; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7893618/)
