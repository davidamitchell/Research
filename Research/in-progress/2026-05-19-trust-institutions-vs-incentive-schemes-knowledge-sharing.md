# Why Do Trust-Based Institutions Outperform Incentive Schemes for Knowledge Sharing?

## Research Question

Why do explicit transactional incentives for sharing often decay or backfire, while trust-based institutions, stable rules and norms that make repeated sharing safe and expected, sustain lower long-run knowledge-sharing costs?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Comparative effects of extrinsic incentives versus relational norms on knowledge sharing.
- Gaming, signalling distortion, and displacement of voluntary motivation in incentive-led schemes.
- Mechanisms through which trust lowers repeated search, help-seeking, and reuse costs.

**Out of scope:**
- Compensation benchmarking and salary-market analysis.
- General employee-engagement interventions unrelated to knowledge transfer.

**Constraints:** Focus on evidence from organisational psychology, research on how rules and norms shape repeated exchange, and field studies in workplace settings.

## Context

Explicit reward schemes can raise visible sharing activity without lowering the deeper interpersonal and interpretive costs that determine whether workers keep asking, explaining, and reusing knowledge over time, so the core design question is whether durable sharing depends more on price signals or on trusted social institutions. [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/oup/qjecon/v115y2000i3p791-810..html; https://home.ubalt.edu/tmitch/642/Articles%20syllabus/Deci%20Koestner%20Ryan%20meta%20IM%20psy%20bull%2099.pdf; https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/2025_JinPeiEtAl_PayForIndividual.pdf; https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f]

## Approach

1. Compare short-run and long-run outcomes of incentive-led versus trust-led sharing systems.
2. Identify failure modes of explicit incentives, including opportunism and signalling distortions.
3. Define institutional conditions that make trust effects durable and scalable.

### §2 Investigation

#### Prior completed-item sweep

- [fact; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html] The completed low-friction-pathways item argues that knowledge routes are sustained when workers experience the authoritative path as cheaper across search, access, interpretation, and social-risk dimensions.
- [fact; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-how-do-activation-energy-barriers-shape-knowledge-seeking-behaviour.html] The completed activation-energy item finds that help-seeking stalls when expertise is hard to identify, hard to reach, or socially risky to ask for, and that psychologically safe, predictable access routines lower those barriers.
- [fact; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-03-12-exploration-synthesis-gap.html] The completed exploration-synthesis-gap item reports that high pay for individual performance reduces knowledge sharing and that competitive credit environments suppress synthesis and reuse.

#### A. What happens when extrinsic incentives are introduced?

- [fact; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/oup/qjecon/v115y2000i3p791-810..html] Gneezy and Rustichini report that monetary compensation does not improve performance monotonically: larger amounts improve performance relative to smaller amounts, but offering money can produce worse performance than offering no compensation.
- [fact; source: https://home.ubalt.edu/tmitch/642/Articles%20syllabus/Deci%20Koestner%20Ryan%20meta%20IM%20psy%20bull%2099.pdf] Deci, Koestner, and Ryan's meta-analysis of 128 experiments finds that expected tangible rewards, including engagement-contingent, completion-contingent, and performance-contingent rewards, significantly undermine free-choice intrinsic motivation.
- [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/oup/qjecon/v115y2000i3p791-810..html; https://home.ubalt.edu/tmitch/642/Articles%20syllabus/Deci%20Koestner%20Ryan%20meta%20IM%20psy%20bull%2099.pdf] The shared mechanism is not that all rewards fail, but that rewards can recode a previously voluntary act into monitored compliance, which makes low or controlling incentives especially fragile over time.

#### B. What direct evidence exists for knowledge sharing rather than generic task performance?

- [fact; source: https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/2025_JinPeiEtAl_PayForIndividual.pdf] Jin and colleagues study 385 employees across eight Chinese firms and report that pay for individual performance has an inverted U-shaped effect on intrinsic motivation, which in turn affects knowledge sharing.
- [fact; source: https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/2025_JinPeiEtAl_PayForIndividual.pdf] The same study argues that low to moderate reward intensity can be interpreted as informational, while medium to high intensity becomes more controlling and damages autonomy and self-competence.
- [fact; source: https://core.ac.uk/display/30042414] Cabrera and Cabrera describe knowledge sharing as a shared-benefit dilemma in which contributing personal insight can carry an individual cost even when the group benefits, and they group viable interventions into payoff restructuring, efficacy building, and making group identity and personal responsibility more salient.
- [inference; source: https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/2025_JinPeiEtAl_PayForIndividual.pdf; https://core.ac.uk/display/30042414; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-03-12-exploration-synthesis-gap.html] Incentive-led knowledge-sharing programmes are therefore unstable when they rely mainly on visible individual payoffs, because the same scheme that initially activates contribution can later intensify competition, bargaining, and selective withholding.

#### C. What makes trust-based institutions durable?

- [fact; source: https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f] Edmondson defines psychological safety as a shared belief that the team is safe for interpersonal risk taking and reports that, across 51 work teams, psychological safety is associated with learning behavior while team efficacy is not once psychological safety is controlled for.
- [fact; source: https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f] Edmondson also reports that learning behavior mediates between psychological safety and team performance, and that context support and team leader coaching shape team outcomes alongside shared beliefs.
- [fact; source: https://digitalcommons.odu.edu/management_fac_pubs/13/] Frazier and colleagues aggregate 136 independent samples representing more than 22,000 individuals and nearly 5,000 groups and conclude that psychological safety has a broad network of antecedents and outcomes that includes task performance and organisational citizenship behavior beyond related constructs.
- [fact; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-how-do-activation-energy-barriers-shape-knowledge-seeking-behaviour.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html] The adjacent completed items show that trusted knowledge pathways are used when expertise is visible, access is predictable, and asking does not carry a competence or status penalty.
- [inference; source: https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://digitalcommons.odu.edu/management_fac_pubs/13/; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-how-do-activation-energy-barriers-shape-knowledge-seeking-behaviour.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html] Trust-based institutions lower repeat interaction cost because they remove status risk from asking, reduce the need to renegotiate reciprocity each time, and make it easier to judge where trustworthy help resides.

#### D. What failure modes separate incentive schemes from trust-based institutions?

- [fact; source: https://core.ac.uk/display/30042414] Cabrera and Cabrera's shared-benefit-dilemma framing implies that individual contributors can rationally hold back when the private cost of sharing exceeds the perceived group return.
- [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/oup/qjecon/v115y2000i3p791-810..html; https://home.ubalt.edu/tmitch/642/Articles%20syllabus/Deci%20Koestner%20Ryan%20meta%20IM%20psy%20bull%2099.pdf; https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/2025_JinPeiEtAl_PayForIndividual.pdf] Incentive schemes decay when they make counting contributions easier than transferring judgment, because the visible token becomes the rewarded unit while tacit explanation, careful interpretation, and reuse remain under-rewarded.
- [inference; source: https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/2025_JinPeiEtAl_PayForIndividual.pdf; https://core.ac.uk/display/30042414; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-03-12-exploration-synthesis-gap.html] Opportunism and gaming are not separate edge cases from incentive design, they are the predictable result of high-visibility, individualised schemes that attach scarce recognition to public artefacts and weak recognition to collective sensemaking.
- [inference; source: https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://digitalcommons.odu.edu/management_fac_pubs/13/; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-how-do-activation-energy-barriers-shape-knowledge-seeking-behaviour.html] Trust-based institutions scale more durably when they embed safe asking, mentoring, and low-friction access into ordinary work rather than presenting sharing as an optional extra contest for rewards.

### §3 Reasoning

- [fact; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/oup/qjecon/v115y2000i3p791-810..html; https://home.ubalt.edu/tmitch/642/Articles%20syllabus/Deci%20Koestner%20Ryan%20meta%20IM%20psy%20bull%2099.pdf; https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/2025_JinPeiEtAl_PayForIndividual.pdf] The incentive evidence is consistent on direction but not on a simple binary rule: small or controlling rewards can worsen outcomes, while moderate performance-linked rewards can help until they become controlling.
- [fact; source: https://core.ac.uk/display/30042414; https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://digitalcommons.odu.edu/management_fac_pubs/13/] The trust literature is also not a vague culture claim, because it specifies observable mechanisms, lower interpersonal risk, stronger learning behavior, and greater willingness to contribute inside a collective setting.
- [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/oup/qjecon/v115y2000i3p791-810..html; https://home.ubalt.edu/tmitch/642/Articles%20syllabus/Deci%20Koestner%20Ryan%20meta%20IM%20psy%20bull%2099.pdf; https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/2025_JinPeiEtAl_PayForIndividual.pdf; https://core.ac.uk/display/30042414; https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f] The most defensible synthesis is that incentive schemes are activation tools with a narrow operating window, while trust-based institutions are cost-reduction tools for repeated exchange.
- [inference; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-how-do-activation-energy-barriers-shape-knowledge-seeking-behaviour.html] That distinction matters because knowledge sharing is a repeated coordination problem rather than a one-off output problem, so long-run performance depends on whether the next exchange remains cheap, safe, and easy to interpret.

### §4 Consistency Check

- [fact; source: https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/2025_JinPeiEtAl_PayForIndividual.pdf] Jin and colleagues explicitly show a nonlinear pattern, so any claim that incentives always backfire would overstate the evidence.
- [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/oup/qjecon/v115y2000i3p791-810..html; https://home.ubalt.edu/tmitch/642/Articles%20syllabus/Deci%20Koestner%20Ryan%20meta%20IM%20psy%20bull%2099.pdf; https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/2025_JinPeiEtAl_PayForIndividual.pdf] The tension resolves when reward intensity and meaning are separated: moderate rewards can carry informational value, but weak, expected, or highly controlling rewards crowd out voluntary motivation and trust.
- [fact; source: https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://digitalcommons.odu.edu/management_fac_pubs/13/] The psychological-safety evidence does not imply that trust replaces structure, because Edmondson and Frazier both locate outcomes in a wider system that includes leader behavior and contextual support.
- [inference; source: https://core.ac.uk/display/30042414; https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://digitalcommons.odu.edu/management_fac_pubs/13/] No unresolved contradiction remains once the comparison is framed as long-run cost and durability rather than short-run activation alone.

### §5 Depth and Breadth Expansion

**Behavioural lens**

- [fact; source: https://home.ubalt.edu/tmitch/642/Articles%20syllabus/Deci%20Koestner%20Ryan%20meta%20IM%20psy%20bull%2099.pdf; https://ideas.repec.org/a/oup/qjecon/v115y2000i3p791-810..html; https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/2025_JinPeiEtAl_PayForIndividual.pdf] The behavioural pattern is stable across the consulted studies: rewards work differently depending on whether recipients read them as supportive information or as external control.

**Organisational lens**

- [fact; source: https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://digitalcommons.odu.edu/management_fac_pubs/13/; https://core.ac.uk/display/30042414] The organisational mechanism is not only motivation but also climate, because people contribute differently when the local norm says that asking, explaining, and exposing partial knowledge are safe.

**Economic lens**

- [inference; source: https://core.ac.uk/display/30042414; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html] Incentive schemes repeatedly reopen the bargaining problem over who gets rewarded for each contribution, while trust-based institutions amortise that bargaining cost into stable expectations about reciprocity and responsibility.

**Measurement lens**

- [inference; source: https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/2025_JinPeiEtAl_PayForIndividual.pdf; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-how-do-activation-energy-barriers-shape-knowledge-seeking-behaviour.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html] Durable success should be measured through repeat help-seeking, successful reuse, faster time to useful contact, and lower abandonment of hard questions rather than by raw counts of uploads, posts, or points.

## Findings

### Executive Summary

Hypothesis: Trust-based institutions outperform explicit sharing incentives over time because they reduce the interpersonal and interpretive costs of asking, sharing, and reusing knowledge without turning those acts into contested scorekeeping. [inference; source: https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://digitalcommons.odu.edu/management_fac_pubs/13/; https://core.ac.uk/display/30042414; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-how-do-activation-energy-barriers-shape-knowledge-seeking-behaviour.html]

Hypothesis: Explicit incentives can increase visible contribution in a narrow operating window, but small, expected, or highly individualised rewards often crowd out intrinsic motivation and can reduce performance or knowledge sharing relative to moderate reward levels or to no reward at all. [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/oup/qjecon/v115y2000i3p791-810..html; https://home.ubalt.edu/tmitch/642/Articles%20syllabus/Deci%20Koestner%20Ryan%20meta%20IM%20psy%20bull%2099.pdf; https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/2025_JinPeiEtAl_PayForIndividual.pdf]

Hypothesis: The durable mechanism is not goodwill alone, but psychologically safe norms, reciprocal expectations, leader coaching, and low-friction access paths that make repeated exchange cheaper by lowering status risk, hesitation, and search cost. [inference; source: https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://digitalcommons.odu.edu/management_fac_pubs/13/; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-how-do-activation-energy-barriers-shape-knowledge-seeking-behaviour.html]

Hypothesis: The strongest operating-model implication is to keep rewards moderate and indirect while investing more heavily in trusted pathways, mentoring, and measures of actual reuse and problem resolution rather than counts of posts or points. [inference; source: https://core.ac.uk/display/30042414; https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/2025_JinPeiEtAl_PayForIndividual.pdf; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-how-do-activation-energy-barriers-shape-knowledge-seeking-behaviour.html]

### Key Findings

1. **Generic incentive studies show that expected tangible rewards can reduce voluntary effort or intrinsic motivation, which makes reward-led sharing schemes inherently fragile when they depend on voluntary contribution rather than mandatory compliance.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/oup/qjecon/v115y2000i3p791-810..html; https://home.ubalt.edu/tmitch/642/Articles%20syllabus/Deci%20Koestner%20Ryan%20meta%20IM%20psy%20bull%2099.pdf)
2. **In a direct workplace knowledge-sharing study, pay for individual performance improves sharing only up to a point and then reduces intrinsic motivation and sharing as the scheme becomes more controlling.** ([fact]; medium confidence; source: https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/2025_JinPeiEtAl_PayForIndividual.pdf)
3. **Knowledge sharing has the structure of a shared-benefit dilemma, where contributing can be privately costly even when the group benefits, so changing payoffs alone is only one intervention family.** ([fact]; medium confidence; source: https://core.ac.uk/display/30042414)
4. **Team climates where people believe it is safe to take interpersonal risks are associated with learning behavior, and that climate is reinforced by leader coaching and contextual support rather than by incentives alone.** ([fact]; high confidence; source: https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://digitalcommons.odu.edu/management_fac_pubs/13/)
5. **Trust-based institutions outperform incentive schemes in the long run because they lower the recurring search, access, and social effort required for each exchange, especially status risk and hesitation to ask.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://digitalcommons.odu.edu/management_fac_pubs/13/; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-how-do-activation-energy-barriers-shape-knowledge-seeking-behaviour.html)
6. **Incentive-led programmes decay when they reward visible tokens of contribution more easily than tacit explanation, careful interpretation, or reuse, which creates gaming, withholding, and compliance-shaped sharing.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/2025_JinPeiEtAl_PayForIndividual.pdf; https://core.ac.uk/display/30042414; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-03-12-exploration-synthesis-gap.html)
7. **The most durable design is a mixed system that keeps recognition moderate and indirect while making expert access predictable, mentoring available, and psychologically safe asking part of ordinary work.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://core.ac.uk/display/30042414; https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://digitalcommons.odu.edu/management_fac_pubs/13/; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-how-do-activation-energy-barriers-shape-knowledge-seeking-behaviour.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html)
