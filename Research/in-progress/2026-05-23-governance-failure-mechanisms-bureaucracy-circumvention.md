# Failure mechanisms of internal governance controls: bureaucratic inefficiency and informal circumvention in regulated enterprises

## Research Question

Through what mechanisms do internal governance controls in regulated enterprises transition from coordination cost minimisers to sources of bureaucratic inefficiency and informal circumvention, and what observable signals indicate each failure mode is active?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Mechanisms by which well-designed controls degrade: ossification, scope creep, incentive misalignment, and measurability substitution, meaning proxy measures replace the underlying objective
- Informal circumvention patterns: workarounds, shadow processes, and compliance theatre, meaning visible compliance activity that demonstrates diligence without materially improving the underlying control quality, and the conditions under which they emerge
- Oliver Williamson's opportunism concept applied to internal agents gaming governance controls
- Douglass North's concept of informal norms competing with formal rules when formal rules are perceived as illegitimate or excessively costly
- Empirical evidence, including regulatory post-mortems, case studies, and surveys, of governance failure modes in regulated enterprises

**Out of scope:**
- External regulatory failure as the primary object of study
- Cybersecurity controls and information technology-specific governance failures unless they illustrate a general mechanism
- Individual-level psychology of rule-breaking as the main topic

**Constraints:** Evidence from regulated sectors is preferred. Institutional economics, public choice, and organisational sociology are used as explanatory lenses. Time horizon: 1990 to present for empirical evidence.

## Context

Internal governance controls in regulated enterprises only earn their place when the coordination cost they add is lower than the search, monitoring, bargaining, and error-correction cost they prevent. [inference; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-03-02-transaction-costs.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-03-10-nature-of-the-firm-coase-organisations.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-23-governance-controls-effectiveness-conditions.html]

Adjacent completed items in this corpus already show the positive side of the problem, namely the conditions under which controls remain proportionate and enabling, plus the operational symptoms of queue overload, shadow records, and weak decision-right allocation when controls stop working as intended. [fact; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-23-governance-controls-effectiveness-conditions.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-20-hitl-capacity-thresholds-in-banking-compliance.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-09-system-of-record-bypass-control-deficiencies.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-04-26-ai-lowcode-decision-rights-accountability-liability.html]

This item focuses on the negative transition: why controls that once reduced coordination cost harden into low-signal approvals, proxy-metric games, and shadow work, and which observable signals distinguish those failure modes before they become systemic. [inference; source: https://www.federalreserve.gov/publications/files/svb-review-20230428.pdf; https://www.occ.gov/news-issuances/news-releases/2024/nr-occ-2024-76.html; https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/enforcement20240710a.htm]

## Approach

1. Identify and classify failure-mechanism archetypes: bureaucratic ossification, Goodhart-style proxy substitution, scope and complexity creep, incentive misalignment between governance designers and governed parties, and information-asymmetry exploitation by internal agents.
2. For each archetype, trace the causal mechanism using Transaction Cost Economics and institutional economics: which transaction attributes, institutional conditions, or incentive structures trigger the failure?
3. Identify the informal circumvention patterns that correspond to each failure archetype: what workarounds emerge, how are they rationalised, and how do they propagate through organisations?
4. Review empirical evidence, including regulatory post-mortems and organisational case studies, for documented instances of each mechanism.
5. Synthesise observable early warning signals for each failure mode that governance designers and regulators can use to detect deterioration before it becomes systemic.

#### A. Boundary logic and control overhead

- [fact; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-03-02-transaction-costs.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-03-10-nature-of-the-firm-coase-organisations.html] Prior completed items in this corpus, grounded in Coase and Williamson, state that internal coordination is justified only while the marginal cost of organising work internally remains lower than the market or alternative coordination cost the organisation would otherwise bear.
- [fact; source: https://ageconsearch.umn.edu/record/294665/files/ipr018.pdf; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-03-02-transaction-costs.html] Williamson's accessible 1991 working paper says transactions should be aligned with governance structures in a discriminating, transaction-cost-economising way, and it centres relationship-specific investment, uncertainty, and frequency as the main transaction dimensions.
- [inference; source: https://ageconsearch.umn.edu/record/294665/files/ipr018.pdf; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-23-governance-controls-effectiveness-conditions.html] Internal controls become bureaucratic overhead when the organisation stops aligning control intensity to transaction hazard and instead applies expensive review, approval, or documentation routines to low-specificity, repetitive, or easily reversible work.

#### B. Institutional persistence and proxy substitution

- [fact; source: https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511808678] North's Cambridge summary states that institutions are devised to structure human interaction under uncertainty, that they create incentive structures, and that institutional development can follow a path-dependent pattern.
- [inference; source: https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511808678; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-23-governance-controls-effectiveness-conditions.html] In the present question, that path-dependent logic supports the claim that controls persist after their original hazard, because removal requires explicit authority, review, and political willingness while leaving a control in place is often cheaper for the sponsor than taking responsibility for removing it.
- [fact; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6541803/] Fire and Guestrin restate Goodhart's Law as "when a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure" and show the mechanism by which proxy metrics lose value once actors optimise for the metric itself.
- [fact; source: https://www.federalreserve.gov/publications/files/svb-review-20230428.pdf] The Federal Reserve's review of Silicon Valley Bank states that the bank changed its own risk-management assumptions to reduce how interest-rate and liquidity risks were measured rather than fully addressing the underlying risks.
- [inference; source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6541803/; https://www.federalreserve.gov/publications/files/svb-review-20230428.pdf] Measurability substitution is therefore a concrete governance-failure mechanism, not a metaphor, because managers can improve reported proxy indicators while the underlying exposure, challenge quality, or resilience remains weak.

#### C. Workarounds, shadow processes, and coercive bureaucracy

- [fact; source: https://doi.org/10.17705/1CAIS.03455] Alter defines workarounds as adaptations created when existing methods, management mandates, or system designs impede the desired goal, and says the theory is useful for understanding both compliance and noncompliance with management mandates.
- [fact; source: https://patientsafety.pa.gov/ADVISORIES/Pages/201709_Workarounds.aspx] The Pennsylvania Patient Safety Authority defines workarounds as actions used to circumvent or temporarily fix workflow hindrances or system design flaws and says they can either help the immediate task or create new hazards.
- [fact; source: https://qualitysafety.bmj.com/content/34/5/317] Clark et al. reviewed 27 papers covering 21 safety standards and found that workarounds are used frequently in frontline healthcare, with organisational causes reported as the most prominent reason.
- [fact; source: https://www.jstor.org/stable/2393986] Adler and Borys distinguish enabling bureaucracy, meaning formalisation that helps people perform the work, from coercive bureaucracy, meaning formalisation primarily aimed at enforcing compliance and limiting discretion.
- [inference; source: https://doi.org/10.17705/1CAIS.03455; https://patientsafety.pa.gov/ADVISORIES/Pages/201709_Workarounds.aspx; https://qualitysafety.bmj.com/content/34/5/317; https://www.jstor.org/stable/2393986] Informal circumvention is most likely when formal controls become coercive rather than enabling, because staff still face delivery pressure and therefore route around obstacles that slow or block the task without obviously improving decision quality.

#### D. Regulated-enterprise case evidence

- [fact; source: https://www.federalreserve.gov/publications/files/svb-review-20230428.pdf] The Federal Reserve review says Silicon Valley Bank's board and senior management failed to manage basic interest-rate and liquidity risks, failed to build a governance and risk-management framework that kept up with rapid growth, and did not provide the full board with adequate information about core risks.
- [fact; source: https://www.occ.gov/news-issuances/news-releases/2024/nr-occ-2024-76.html] The Office of the Comptroller of the Currency says Citibank remained under amendment because of longstanding deficiencies in enterprise-wide risk management, compliance risk management, data governance, and internal controls, plus lack of sufficient and sustainable progress toward remediation.
- [fact; source: https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/enforcement20240710a.htm] The Federal Reserve Board says Citigroup made insufficient progress remediating data-quality-management problems and failed to implement compensating controls to manage its ongoing risk.
- [inference; source: https://www.federalreserve.gov/publications/files/svb-review-20230428.pdf; https://www.occ.gov/news-issuances/news-releases/2024/nr-occ-2024-76.html; https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/enforcement20240710a.htm] Recent banking cases therefore show three concrete failure mechanisms: controls lag business growth or complexity, management information becomes too weak for effective challenge, and institutions accumulate remediation programs or compensating controls without closing the underlying design gap.

### §6 Synthesis

**Executive summary:**

Internal governance controls in regulated enterprises turn from coordination aids into bureaucracy when their administrative burden, proxy metrics, and approval rituals grow faster than their ability to improve the underlying risk decision, so staff and managers shift effort into workarounds, shadow processes, or nominal review. [inference; source: https://ageconsearch.umn.edu/record/294665/files/ipr018.pdf; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6541803/; https://doi.org/10.17705/1CAIS.03455; https://www.federalreserve.gov/publications/files/svb-review-20230428.pdf]

The supported mechanisms include control misalignment to transaction hazard, path-dependent persistence of old controls, proxy-target substitution, coercive rather than enabling formalisation, and weak management information or compensating controls that hide the real state of the system. [inference; source: https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511808678; https://www.jstor.org/stable/2393986; https://www.occ.gov/news-issuances/news-releases/2024/nr-occ-2024-76.html; https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/enforcement20240710a.htm]

A recurring circumvention pattern is not dramatic rule-breaking but practical rerouting, including shadow records, duplicate manual reconciliation, queue-clearing without meaningful challenge, and local workarounds that solve the task while quietly weakening the formal control surface.
