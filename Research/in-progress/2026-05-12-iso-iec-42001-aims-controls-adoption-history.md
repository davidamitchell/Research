# International Organization for Standardization (ISO) and International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC) 42001:2023 controls, adoption, reputation, and evolution

## Research Question

What is International Organization for Standardization (ISO) and International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC) 42001:2023 for an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS), and which specific controls in Annex A and the main body mitigate key Artificial Intelligence (AI) risks? How widely has the standard been adopted and certified since publication, how is it perceived by enterprises, regulators, certification bodies, and AI ethics experts, and what development milestones, amendments, and planned revisions define its evolution?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- ISO/IEC 42001:2023 purpose, structure, clauses, and Annex A control catalogue.
- Mapping controls to core AI risk classes: bias, transparency, data quality, security, privacy, accountability, and ethical harms.
- Public evidence of adoption and certification activity by region, sector, and organisation type.
- Reputation signals from industry guidance, regulators, accredited certification bodies, and AI governance specialists.
- Development timeline, related standards, and announced updates.

**Out of scope:**
- Certifying or auditing any specific organisation implementation.
- Providing legal advice or jurisdiction-specific compliance determinations.
- Reproducing paywalled standard text beyond publicly available summaries.

**Constraints:** public sources only, primary sources preferred, and post-2023 adoption evidence weighted most heavily.

## Context

- [inference; source: https://www.iso.org/standard/42001; https://www.deloitte.com/uk/en/services/audit-assurance/blogs/navigating-ai-assurance-spotlight-on-iso-iec.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-09-data-governance-standards-ai-agentic-applicability.html] Organisations deciding whether to adopt ISO/IEC 42001 need to know whether it is a practical governance baseline or mostly a signalling device, because management-system certification sits between high-level AI principles and sector-specific operational controls.

## Approach

1. Establish what ISO/IEC 42001:2023 contains, including clause architecture, annexes, and publicly documented Annex A control families.
2. Map main-body requirements and Annex A control families to major AI risks and identify where the standard remains principle-level.
3. Gather public evidence of adoption and certification activity from accredited certification bodies, enterprise disclosures, and public verification infrastructure.
4. Compare enterprise, regulator, accreditation, certification-body, and expert perceptions of the standard.
5. Reconstruct the timeline from Subcommittee 42 (SC 42) formation through publication and companion-standard evolution.

### §2 Investigation

#### A. What ISO/IEC 42001 contains

- [fact; source: https://www.iso.org/standard/42001] ISO describes ISO/IEC 42001:2023 as the first international standard for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS) for organisations that provide or use AI-based products or services.
- [fact; source: https://www.iso.org/home/insights-news/resources/iso-42001-explained-what-it-is.html] ISO's public explainer says the standard covers leadership and organisational context, AI policy and objectives, risk management, data governance and system lifecycle controls, transparency and information provision, performance evaluation, and continual improvement.
- [fact; source: https://www.unido.org/sites/default/files/files/2025-07/Microsoft%20-%20Overview%20of%20ISO%20IEC%2042001.pdf] A public Microsoft presentation of ISO/IEC 42001 shows clauses 4 through 10 as the management-system core and identifies a dedicated requirement for AI system impact assessment alongside AI risk assessment and treatment.
- [fact; source: https://www.unido.org/sites/default/files/files/2025-07/Microsoft%20-%20Overview%20of%20ISO%20IEC%2042001.pdf; https://mindsetcyber.com.au/iso-42001-controls-list/; https://www.isms.online/iso-42001/annex-a-controls/] Publicly accessible secondary mappings agree that Annex A contains 38 controls organised into nine objectives, A.2 through A.10, covering AI policy, internal organisation, resources, impact assessment, AI system lifecycle, data, information for interested parties, use of AI systems, and third-party or customer relationships.
- [fact; source: https://mindsetcyber.com.au/iso-42001-controls-list/; https://www.unido.org/sites/default/files/files/2025-07/Microsoft%20-%20Overview%20of%20ISO%20IEC%2042001.pdf] Public control summaries name representative Annex A controls such as A.5.2 AI system impact assessment process, A.6.2.4 AI system verification and validation, A.6.2.8 AI system recording of event logs, A.7.4 quality of data for AI systems, A.8.2 system documentation and information for users, A.9.2 processes for responsible use of AI systems, and A.10.3 suppliers.
- [fact; source: https://www.iso.org/standard/42001] ISO states that the standard is a management-system standard that uses a Plan-Do-Check-Act method and provides a practical way to manage AI-related risks and opportunities across an organisation rather than certifying the details of a single AI application.

#### B. Which controls mitigate which risks

- [fact; source: https://www.iso.org/home/insights-news/resources/iso-42001-explained-what-it-is.html; https://mindsetcyber.com.au/iso-42001-controls-list/] The main-body requirements and Annex A both explicitly address risk assessment, impact assessment, transparency, data quality, lifecycle management, and stakeholder information, which are the standard's main public control surfaces.
- [inference; source: https://mindsetcyber.com.au/iso-42001-controls-list/; https://www.isms.online/iso-42001/annex-a-controls/; https://www.iso.org/home/insights-news/resources/iso-42001-explained-what-it-is.html] Bias and broader ethical harms are mitigated most directly through Clause 6 and Clause 8 impact assessment duties plus Annex A.5 impact-assessment controls, A.7 data-quality and provenance controls, A.8 information duties, and A.9 responsible-use controls, because those are the public control families that require organisations to identify affected groups, assess foreseeable misuse, document limits, and constrain system use.
- [inference; source: https://mindsetcyber.com.au/iso-42001-controls-list/; https://www.unido.org/sites/default/files/files/2025-07/Microsoft%20-%20Overview%20of%20ISO%20IEC%2042001.pdf; https://www.iso.org/home/insights-news/resources/iso-42001-explained-what-it-is.html] Transparency and accountability are mitigated most directly through Clause 5 policy and roles, Clause 7 documented information, Clause 9 audits and management review, Annex A.3 role assignment, Annex A.6 technical documentation and event logging, and Annex A.8 information for users and other interested parties.
- [inference; source: https://mindsetcyber.com.au/iso-42001-controls-list/; https://www.isms.online/iso-42001/annex-a-controls/; https://www.iso.org/standard/42001] Data quality, privacy, and security risks are mitigated through a combination of Clause 6 and Clause 8 risk processes, Annex A.4 resource documentation, Annex A.6 lifecycle controls, Annex A.7 data provenance and preparation controls, and the standard's intended integration with existing information-security and privacy management systems rather than by a standalone technical benchmark.
- [inference; source: https://www.cloudsecurityalliance.org/articles/understanding-iso-42001-responsible-ai-governance-in-an-evolving-regulatory-landscape; https://www.deloitte.com/uk/en/services/audit-assurance/blogs/navigating-ai-assurance-spotlight-on-iso-iec.html; https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.18670] The standard is strongest at governing organisational process quality, auditability, and due diligence, but weakest as a standalone assurance mechanism for model-specific performance, robustness, or legal compliance, because multiple expert sources describe it as a foundation or bridge rather than a complete substitute for sectoral law or technical testing.

#### C. Adoption and certification activity

- [fact; source: https://www.iso.org/home/insights-news/resources/iso-42001-explained-what-it-is.html] ISO states that certification to ISO/IEC 42001 is voluntary and carried out by independent certification bodies rather than by ISO itself.
- [fact; source: https://www.sac-accreditation.gov.sg/launch-of-iso-iec-42001-artificial-intelligence-management-systems-accreditation-programme/; https://www.iso.org/standard/42006] Accreditation infrastructure is maturing: Singapore's accreditation council launched an ISO/IEC 42001 programme in February 2025, and ISO/IEC 42006:2025 was published in July 2025 to define additional requirements for bodies that audit and certify AIMS.
- [fact; source: https://scresources.rina.org/resources/Documents/ISO-42001-appendix.pdf] Registro Italiano Navale (RINA)'s March 2024 appendix shows certification bodies were already operationalising ISO/IEC 42001 against draft International Standard (DIS) 42006 requirements before the final publication of ISO/IEC 42006.
- [fact; source: https://www.sgs.com/en-us/news/2025/09/iso-iec-42001-trustworthy-certification-in-the-age-of-ai; https://www.schellman.com/blog/iso-certifications/iso-42001-lessons-learned; https://assets.kpmg.com/content/dam/kpmgsites/ch/pdf/ISOIEC-42001-certification.pdf.coredownload.inline.pdf] Multiple certification bodies and assessment firms, including Societe Generale de Surveillance (SGS), Schellman, and Klynveld Peat Marwick Goerdeler (KPMG) Switzerland, publicly market ISO/IEC 42001 certification services and describe growing demand tied to regulation, supply-chain assurance, and customer trust.
- [fact; source: https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/compliance/regulatory/offering-iso-42001; https://www.sap.com/about/trust-center/certification-compliance.html] Public enterprise adoption exists: Microsoft states that several Copilot and related AI services are in scope for ISO/IEC 42001 certification, and Systems, Applications, and Products in Data Processing (SAP) lists ISO/IEC 42001 AI management system compliance in its public trust-center catalogue.
- [fact; source: https://www.iafcertsearch.org/search/certified-entities] Public certificate-verification infrastructure exists through International Accreditation Forum (IAF) CertSearch, which presents itself as a global database for accredited management-system certification validation.
- [inference; source: https://www.iafcertsearch.org/search/certified-entities; https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/compliance/regulatory/offering-iso-42001; https://www.sap.com/about/trust-center/certification-compliance.html; https://www.schellman.com/blog/iso-certifications/iso-42001-lessons-learned] Public evidence supports an "early but real" adoption reading rather than a mass-adoption reading, because accessible sources show maturing audit infrastructure and named certified enterprises but do not expose a simple, comprehensive public count of certified organisations by sector or region.

#### D. Perception and reputation

- [inference; source: https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/compliance/regulatory/offering-iso-42001; https://www.sap.com/about/trust-center/certification-compliance.html] Enterprise vendors frame ISO/IEC 42001 as a trust, transparency, and compliance signal that reassures customers about responsible AI management rather than as proof that every AI output is correct.
- [inference; source: https://www.sgs.com/en-us/news/2025/09/iso-iec-42001-trustworthy-certification-in-the-age-of-ai; https://www.schellman.com/blog/iso-certifications/iso-42001-lessons-learned; https://www.cloudsecurityalliance.org/articles/understanding-iso-42001-responsible-ai-governance-in-an-evolving-regulatory-landscape] Certification and assurance specialists present ISO/IEC 42001 as a way to turn broad responsible-AI claims into auditable governance evidence, especially around risk management, lifecycle discipline, and accredited third-party review.
- [inference; source: https://www.deloitte.com/uk/en/services/audit-assurance/blogs/navigating-ai-assurance-spotlight-on-iso-iec.html; https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.18670; https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.17374] Governance researchers and compliance-focused experts treat ISO/IEC 42001 as a foundation or bridge rather than a complete end state, because they pair it with the European Union (EU) Artificial Intelligence Act, National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) guidance, or structured impact-assessment practices instead of treating certification alone as sufficient.
- [inference; source: https://www.bsi.bund.de/EN/Themen/Unternehmen-und-Organisationen/Informationen-und-Empfehlungen/Kuenstliche-Intelligenz/AIC4/aic4_node.html] The German Federal Office for Information Security uses a separate criteria catalogue for secure Artificial Intelligence (AI) cloud services that covers robustness, performance, reliability, data quality, explainability, and bias, which suggests that public-sector evaluators still rely on more specific overlays alongside general management-system approaches.
- [inference; source: https://www.cloudsecurityalliance.org/articles/understanding-iso-42001-responsible-ai-governance-in-an-evolving-regulatory-landscape; https://www.deloitte.com/uk/en/services/audit-assurance/blogs/navigating-ai-assurance-spotlight-on-iso-iec.html; https://www.bsi.bund.de/EN/Themen/Unternehmen-und-Organisationen/Informationen-und-Empfehlungen/Kuenstliche-Intelligenz/AIC4/aic4_node.html] The reputation picture is therefore positive but qualified: the consulted expert and public-authority sources treat ISO/IEC 42001 as a credible baseline for governance maturity, yet they still expect additional technical, legal, or sector-specific assurance layers.

#### E. History and evolution

- [fact; source: https://www.iso.org/committee/6794475.html] ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 42, the committee responsible for AI standards work, was created in 2017.
- [fact; source: https://www.iso.org/standard/42001] ISO/IEC 42001 first edition was published in December 2023.
- [fact; source: https://www.iso.org/standard/42006] ISO/IEC 42006 first edition was published in July 2025 to define additional requirements for AIMS certification bodies.
- [fact; source: https://www.iso.org/standard/42001] ISO's public ISO/IEC 42001 product page now packages ISO/IEC 42001 together with ISO/IEC 42005, which ISO describes as a responsible-AI governance and impact standard.
- [inference; source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.18670] Academic commentary published in late 2024 already treated ISO/IEC 42001 as a foundation for implementing or nationalising more specific compliance structures under the EU Artificial Intelligence Act, which suggests that the standard's evolution is already being shaped by downstream regulatory alignment work.

### §5 Depth and Breadth Expansion

- [inference; source: https://mindsetcyber.com.au/iso-42001-controls-list/; https://www.unido.org/sites/default/files/files/2025-07/Microsoft%20-%20Overview%20of%20ISO%20IEC%2042001.pdf] From a technical lens, the most operationally important public controls are impact assessment, lifecycle documentation, verification and validation, event logging, data provenance, and intended-use controls, because those mechanisms create evidence that later audits and incident reviews can examine.
- [inference; source: https://www.deloitte.com/uk/en/services/audit-assurance/blogs/navigating-ai-assurance-spotlight-on-iso-iec.html; https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.18670; https://www.sac-accreditation.gov.sg/launch-of-iso-iec-42001-artificial-intelligence-management-systems-accreditation-programme/] From a regulatory lens, regulators and accreditation bodies appear to value ISO/IEC 42001 most as a governance bridge that can be aligned with harder legal obligations rather than as a replacement for those obligations.
- [inference; source: https://assets.kpmg.com/content/dam/kpmgsites/ch/pdf/ISOIEC-42001-certification.pdf.coredownload.inline.pdf; https://www.sgs.com/en-us/news/2025/09/iso-iec-42001-trustworthy-certification-in-the-age-of-ai; https://www.schellman.com/blog/iso-certifications/iso-42001-lessons-learned] From an economic lens, the standard is being sold as a procurement and trust signal, which means adoption can advance before regulators mandate it if customers and large-platform ecosystems start expecting comparable governance evidence.
- [inference; source: https://www.iso.org/committee/6794475.html; https://www.iso.org/standard/42001; https://www.iso.org/standard/42006] From a historical lens, the standard moved from committee formation in 2017 to first publication in 2023 and to certification-body standardisation in 2025, which indicates a compressed timetable for a new international management-system standard.
- [inference; source: https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/compliance/regulatory/offering-iso-42001; https://www.sap.com/about/trust-center/certification-compliance.html; https://www.cloudsecurityalliance.org/articles/understanding-iso-42001-responsible-ai-governance-in-an-evolving-regulatory-landscape] From a behavioural lens, buyers and executives are likely to use ISO/IEC 42001 as shorthand for a vendor having a serious Artificial Intelligence (AI) governance programme, even though the underlying assurance is about management-system discipline rather than guaranteed output quality.

### §6 Synthesis

**Executive summary:**

ISO/IEC 42001:2023 is a useful but incomplete governance baseline for organisational AI, because it converts AI risk, lifecycle, accountability, and stakeholder-information duties into a certifiable management system without certifying model outputs themselves. [inference; source: https://www.iso.org/standard/42001; https://www.cloudsecurityalliance.org/articles/understanding-iso-42001-responsible-ai-governance-in-an-evolving-regulatory-landscape; https://www.deloitte.com/uk/en/services/audit-assurance/blogs/navigating-ai-assurance-spotlight-on-iso-iec.html] Publicly accessible material shows a main-body structure centred on leadership, planning, support, operation, performance evaluation, and improvement, plus an Annex A catalogue of 38 controls across nine objectives that collectively target impact assessment, lifecycle governance, data quality, transparency, intended use, and third-party accountability.
