# How Do Activation-Energy Barriers, the threshold costs of starting a knowledge request, Shape Knowledge-Seeking Behaviour?

## Research Question

How do initiation costs such as evaluation anxiety, search effort, and access uncertainty act as a threshold barrier that suppresses knowledge seeking, and which organisational routines lower that threshold most reliably?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Behavioural and social initiation costs that discourage employees from asking for help.
- Organisational routines, including office hours, peer mentoring, and expert directories, as barrier-lowering mechanisms.
- Observable indicators for reduced initiation cost, including faster first contact and higher help-seeking frequency.

**Out of scope:**
- Tool-specific user-interface optimisation of knowledge management platforms.
- Financial return on investment modelling for knowledge-sharing interventions.

**Constraints:** Focus on evidence from organisational behaviour and knowledge-management research with practical interventions.

## Context

[fact; source: https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-03-15-adam-smith-org-design-desire-paths-ai.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-17-ai-policy-ambiguity-institutional-knowledge-social-friction-risk.html] Prior completed repository work shows that workers route around high-friction formal paths, that low-effort pathways often dominate before relevance is evaluated fully, and that social friction can suppress consultation even when information exists elsewhere in the organisation.

## Approach

1. Identify and classify initiation barriers in internal knowledge seeking.
2. Evaluate intervention designs that reduce initiation friction without creating heavy process overhead.
3. Define practical metrics to test whether initiation-cost reduction is durable over time.

### §2 Investigation

#### A. What makes a knowledge request stall before first contact?

- [fact; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html] Borgatti and Cross propose and test a formal information-seeking model in which the probability of seeking information from another person depends on knowing what that person knows, valuing what that person knows, gaining timely access to that person's thinking, and perceiving that asking will not be too costly.
- [fact; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html] The same study reports strong support for the knowing, value, and access variables across two research sites, while the generic cost variable receives weaker support than the other three factors.
- [fact; source: https://colab.ws/articles/10.1006/obhd.1997.2746; https://doi.org/10.1177/0021886302381002] Lee's help-seeking research finds that people may avoid asking for help even when help is needed and available, because asking can imply incompetence, dependence, inferiority, or powerlessness.
- [fact; source: https://colab.ws/articles/10.1006/obhd.1997.2746] Lee's 1997 study reports more help-seeking between equal-status than unequal-status individuals, and shows that organisational norms change how strongly status concerns suppress asking.
- [fact; source: https://doi.org/10.1177/0021886302381002] Lee's 2002 field study reports lower help-seeking when the seeker is male, when the occupation is male-oriented, and when the task is novel and central to organisational core competence, with perceived social costs mediating these effects.
- [fact; source: https://ideas.repec.org/p/tin/wpaper/20090042.html] Swank's consulting model shows that even in high-risk settings where consultation reduces error, junior professionals may still avoid consulting supervisors because consultation can be read as weakness rather than strength.
- [fact; source: https://doi.org/10.1002/smj.4250171105] Szulanski's study of internal best-practice transfer finds that internal stickiness is driven mainly by the recipient's limited ability to absorb the practice, unclear understanding of what makes the practice work, and arduous source-recipient relationships rather than by motivation alone.
- [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://doi.org/10.1177/0021886302381002; https://ideas.repec.org/p/tin/wpaper/20090042.html; https://doi.org/10.1002/smj.4250171105] Taken together, the strongest initiation barriers are uncertainty about who can help, uncertainty about whether help will be worth the interruption, delay or access friction, and the social risk of looking weak or ill-prepared when asking.

#### B. Which routines lower those specific costs?

- [fact; source: https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f] Edmondson defines psychological safety as a shared belief that the team is safe for interpersonal risk taking and reports that, across 51 work teams, psychological safety is associated with learning behavior while team efficacy is not once psychological safety is controlled for.
- [fact; source: https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f] Edmondson also reports that team structures such as context support and team leader coaching shape learning outcomes together with shared beliefs.
- [fact; source: https://digitalcommons.odu.edu/management_fac_pubs/13/] Frazier and colleagues aggregate 136 independent samples covering more than 22,000 individuals and nearly 5,000 groups and conclude that psychological safety has a broad antecedent and outcome network that includes task performance and organisational citizenship behavior beyond adjacent concepts.
- [fact; source: https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439] Bryant's peer-mentoring study reports that a peer mentor training course increased perceived peer mentor knowledge and skills, and that higher perceived peer mentoring was associated with higher perceived knowledge creation and sharing in a software firm.
- [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439] Expert directories, expertise maps, and scheduled access windows are high-fit barrier-lowering routines because they target the two strongest mechanism variables directly, namely knowing who knows what and timely access to that person's thinking.
- [inference; source: https://doi.org/10.1177/0021886302381002; https://colab.ws/articles/10.1006/obhd.1997.2746; https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f] Leader behaviors and local rules that normalise questions lower the interpersonal price of asking, because status and image costs matter most when the question is visible, novel, central, or directed across a hierarchy.
- [inference; source: https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439; https://doi.org/10.1002/smj.4250171105] Peer mentoring does more than create safe access, because it also reduces interpretation burden by giving seekers a known relationship through which tacit or ambiguous practice can be translated into usable guidance.

#### C. How should durable barrier reduction be measured?

- [fact; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html] Borgatti and Cross treat information seeking as an observable choice process, which supports using actual help-seeking frequency and access outcomes as performance-relevant indicators rather than relying only on attitudinal self-reports.
- [fact; source: https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://digitalcommons.odu.edu/management_fac_pubs/13/] The psychological-safety literature links lower interpersonal risk to learning behavior and performance, which supports measuring barrier reduction through both initiation behavior and downstream learning signals.
- [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html] The most useful leading indicators are time to first useful contact, query abandonment before contact, cross-boundary help-seeking frequency, successful referral completion, peer-mentoring reuse, and reuse of canonical sources after consultation.

### §3 Reasoning

- [fact; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://doi.org/10.1177/0021886302381002; https://doi.org/10.1002/smj.4250171105] The direct evidence is strongest for three barrier families, expertise visibility, access predictability, and social-image risk, with knowledge complexity acting as a fourth amplifier when the practice is hard to decode.
- [fact; source: https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://digitalcommons.odu.edu/management_fac_pubs/13; https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439] The direct intervention evidence is strongest for psychologically safe team conditions and peer mentoring, while evidence for any single expert-directory or office-hours format is more mechanism-driven than format-specific.
- [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439] The synthesis therefore should rank routines by the barrier component they reduce most directly, not by whether the routine label sounds modern or familiar.

### §4 Consistency Check

- [fact; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html] Borgatti and Cross report weaker support for a generic undifferentiated cost variable than for knowing, value, and access.
- [inference; source: https://colab.ws/articles/10.1006/obhd.1997.2746; https://doi.org/10.1177/0021886302381002; https://ideas.repec.org/p/tin/wpaper/20090042.html] That result does not weaken the barrier argument, because the help-seeking literature shows that some costs are highly specific, especially status asymmetry, evaluation anxiety, and the fear that asking will signal weakness.
- [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://doi.org/10.1177/0021886302381002; https://doi.org/10.1002/smj.4250171105] The contradiction resolves when initiation cost is treated as multi-dimensional rather than scalar, with knowledge discovery, access, social image, and interpretation burden each able to block first contact independently.
- [inference; source: https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://digitalcommons.odu.edu/management_fac_pubs/13; https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439] Overall confidence remains medium because the mechanism evidence is strong, but the consulted literature does not provide a single head-to-head trial comparing expert directories, office hours, mentoring, and leader coaching inside the same organisation.

### §5 Depth and Breadth Expansion

**Behavioural lens**

- [fact; source: https://colab.ws/articles/10.1006/obhd.1997.2746; https://doi.org/10.1177/0021886302381002] Help-seeking is partly an impression-management problem, not only an information problem, because people avoid asking when the request threatens status, competence image, or power.

**Organisational lens**

- [fact; source: https://doi.org/10.1002/smj.4250171105; https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html] Knowledge pathways fail early when organisations leave expertise too opaque, too hard to reach, or too hard to interpret at the moment of need.

**Design lens**

- [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439] The routine stack with the best evidence fit is visible expertise, predictable access, and socially safe asking, which maps naturally onto expertise maps, scheduled access windows, peer mentors, and leader coaching norms.

**Measurement lens**

- [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html] Durable reduction should appear first in faster first useful contact and lower abandonment, then in higher cross-boundary seeking and higher reuse of authoritative sources after contact.

### §6 Synthesis

#### Executive Summary

Internal knowledge seeking falls sharply when employees cannot quickly identify a credible helper, reach that person predictably, or ask without paying a competence or status penalty. [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://colab.ws/articles/10.1006/obhd.1997.2746; https://doi.org/10.1177/0021886302381002; https://ideas.repec.org/p/tin/wpaper/20090042.html] Across the consulted studies, routines lower that threshold most reliably when they make expertise visible, access predictable, and help-seeking socially safe, rather than when they merely encourage employees to share more. [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://digitalcommons.odu.edu/management_fac_pubs/13; https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439] Peer mentoring and psychologically safe leader coaching have the strongest direct intervention support, while expert directories and scheduled office hours are strong design inferences because they directly target the discovery and timely-access variables that predict seeking. [inference; source: https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439; https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html] A defensible operating model is therefore a layered routine stack, expertise maps, named access windows, peer mentors, and leader behavior that treats questions as normal work, measured by time to first useful contact and successful help-seeking over time. [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html]

#### Key Findings

1. **Internal knowledge seeking is most likely to stall before first contact when the seeker does not know who has the relevant expertise, cannot judge its value, or cannot reach that person quickly enough.** ([fact]; medium confidence; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html)
2. **Social-image costs suppress asking even when help is available, because help-seeking can signal incompetence, dependence, inferiority, or weakness, especially across status differences or on novel core tasks.** ([fact]; medium confidence; source: https://colab.ws/articles/10.1006/obhd.1997.2746; https://doi.org/10.1177/0021886302381002; https://ideas.repec.org/p/tin/wpaper/20090042.html)
3. **Internal transfer barriers are not only motivational, because knowledge seeking also stalls when practices are hard to decode, recipients struggle to absorb them, or source-recipient relationships are arduous.** ([fact]; medium confidence; source: https://doi.org/10.1002/smj.4250171105)
4. **Psychological safety, a shared belief that the team is safe for interpersonal risk taking, and leader coaching directly support learning behavior and therefore lower the interpersonal threshold for asking.** ([fact]; high confidence; source: https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://digitalcommons.odu.edu/management_fac_pubs/13/)
5. **Peer mentoring is the strongest directly supported routine in the consulted intervention literature, because mentor training increased perceived mentor skill and stronger peer mentoring was associated with higher knowledge creation and sharing.** ([fact]; medium confidence; source: https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439)
6. **Expert directories and scheduled office hours are best treated as mechanism-driven routines rather than proven universal fixes, because they directly lower expertise-discovery and timely-access costs identified by the information-seeking model.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439)
7. **Durable barrier reduction should be judged by operational leading indicators, such as time to first useful contact, cross-boundary help-seeking frequency, query abandonment, mentoring reuse, and successful referral completion, not by repository usage alone.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html)

#### Assumptions

- [assumption; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439] Scheduled office hours are treated as a high-fit routine for lowering access uncertainty because making expert availability explicit should reduce the timely-access barrier even though no consulted study isolates office hours alone.
- [assumption; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f] Metrics such as first useful contact and query abandonment are treated as proxies for initiation cost because the consulted literature is stronger on mechanisms and learning outcomes than on a single validated enterprise barrier score.

#### Analysis

The consulted evidence rules out the simple claim that employees fail to seek knowledge mainly because they lack motivation. [inference; source: https://doi.org/10.1002/smj.4250171105; https://colab.ws/articles/10.1006/obhd.1997.2746; https://doi.org/10.1177/0021886302381002] Szulanski shows that transfer barriers are heavily knowledge-related, while the Lee and Swank studies show that seekers can avoid asking even when help is needed because the act of asking is socially costly. [fact; source: https://doi.org/10.1002/smj.4250171105; https://colab.ws/articles/10.1006/obhd.1997.2746; https://ideas.repec.org/p/tin/wpaper/20090042.html] That means the practical design problem is to lower the threshold at three points at once, discovery, access, and interpersonal safety. [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f] A plausible rival explanation is that better knowledge repositories alone could solve the problem, but the evidence weighs against that narrow remedy because the strongest predictors of seeking still include knowing who knows what, timely access to that person, and the cost of interruption or status exposure. [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://colab.ws/articles/10.1006/obhd.1997.2746] Another rival explanation is that any barrier-lowering routine is interchangeable with any other, yet the evidence suggests a layered stack works better because peer mentoring and psychologically safe leader coaching reduce social and interpretive cost directly, while directories and office hours mainly reduce discovery and access cost. [inference; source: https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439; https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html]

#### Risks, Gaps, and Uncertainties

- [fact; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439] The consulted literature does not include a single head-to-head field experiment that compares expert directories, office hours, peer mentoring, and leader coaching inside the same organisation.

## Findings

### Executive Summary

Internal knowledge seeking falls sharply when employees cannot quickly identify a credible helper, reach that person predictably, or ask without paying a competence or status penalty. [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://colab.ws/articles/10.1006/obhd.1997.2746; https://doi.org/10.1177/0021886302381002; https://ideas.repec.org/p/tin/wpaper/20090042.html] Across the consulted studies, routines lower that threshold most reliably when they make expertise visible, access predictable, and help-seeking socially safe, rather than when they merely encourage employees to share more. [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://digitalcommons.odu.edu/management_fac_pubs/13; https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439] Peer mentoring and psychologically safe leader coaching have the strongest direct intervention support, while expert directories and scheduled office hours are strong design inferences because they directly target the discovery and timely-access variables that predict seeking. [inference; source: https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439; https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html] A defensible operating model is therefore a layered routine stack, expertise maps, named access windows, peer mentors, and leader behavior that treats questions as normal work, measured by time to first useful contact and successful help-seeking over time. [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html]

### Key Findings

1. **Internal knowledge seeking is most likely to stall before first contact when the seeker does not know who has the relevant expertise, cannot judge its value, or cannot reach that person quickly enough.** ([fact]; medium confidence; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html)
2. **Social-image costs suppress asking even when help is available, because help-seeking can signal incompetence, dependence, inferiority, or weakness, especially across status differences or on novel core tasks.** ([fact]; medium confidence; source: https://colab.ws/articles/10.1006/obhd.1997.2746; https://doi.org/10.1177/0021886302381002; https://ideas.repec.org/p/tin/wpaper/20090042.html)
3. **Internal transfer barriers are not only motivational, because knowledge seeking also stalls when practices are hard to decode, recipients struggle to absorb them, or source-recipient relationships are arduous.** ([fact]; medium confidence; source: https://doi.org/10.1002/smj.4250171105)
4. **Psychological safety, a shared belief that the team is safe for interpersonal risk taking, and leader coaching directly support learning behavior and therefore lower the interpersonal threshold for asking.** ([fact]; high confidence; source: https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://digitalcommons.odu.edu/management_fac_pubs/13/)
5. **Peer mentoring is the strongest directly supported routine in the consulted intervention literature, because mentor training increased perceived mentor skill and stronger peer mentoring was associated with higher knowledge creation and sharing.** ([fact]; medium confidence; source: https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439)
6. **Expert directories and scheduled office hours are best treated as mechanism-driven routines rather than proven universal fixes, because they directly lower expertise-discovery and timely-access costs identified by the information-seeking model.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439)
7. **Durable barrier reduction should be judged by operational leading indicators, such as time to first useful contact, cross-boundary help-seeking frequency, query abandonment, mentoring reuse, and successful referral completion, not by repository usage alone.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-05-19-align-strategic-relevance-with-low-effort-knowledge-pathways.html)

### Analysis

The consulted evidence rules out the simple claim that employees fail to seek knowledge mainly because they lack motivation. [inference; source: https://doi.org/10.1002/smj.4250171105; https://colab.ws/articles/10.1006/obhd.1997.2746; https://doi.org/10.1177/0021886302381002] Szulanski shows that transfer barriers are heavily knowledge-related, while the Lee and Swank studies show that seekers can avoid asking even when help is needed because the act of asking is socially costly. [fact; source: https://doi.org/10.1002/smj.4250171105; https://colab.ws/articles/10.1006/obhd.1997.2746; https://ideas.repec.org/p/tin/wpaper/20090042.html] That means the practical design problem is to lower the threshold at three points at once, discovery, access, and interpersonal safety. [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f] A plausible rival explanation is that better knowledge repositories alone could solve the problem, but the evidence weighs against that narrow remedy because the strongest predictors of seeking still include knowing who knows what, timely access to that person, and the cost of interruption or status exposure. [inference; source: https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html; https://colab.ws/articles/10.1006/obhd.1997.2746] Another rival explanation is that any barrier-lowering routine is interchangeable with any other, yet the evidence suggests a layered stack works better because peer mentoring and psychologically safe leader coaching reduce social and interpretive cost directly, while directories and office hours mainly reduce discovery and access cost. [inference; source: https://doi.org/10.1177/1059601103258439; https://dash.harvard.edu/entities/publication/13a7b031-0fdd-45ec-a7e0-2b80e2bc679f; https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ormnsc/v49y2003i4p432-445.html]
