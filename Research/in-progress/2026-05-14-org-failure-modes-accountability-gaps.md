# Organisational failure modes: overlapping and absent accountability at strategic and information technology (IT) layers

## Research Question

What failure modes have been empirically observed in organisations where accountability is either overlapping, two or more parties hold the same accountability, or absent, no party owns a given area, at the strategic leadership level, the information technology (IT) delivery layer, or both simultaneously?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Empirically documented failure modes arising from duplicate or missing accountability in IT governance contexts
- Strategic-layer manifestations, such as two senior leaders owning the same portfolio outcome
- IT-delivery-layer manifestations, such as no single team owning a shared service
- Combined cases where gaps exist at both layers concurrently
- Observable symptoms, including decision paralysis, conflicting priorities, finger-pointing, unowned technical debt, scope creep, and initiative abandonment
- Governance models, including Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed (RACI) and Driver, Approver, Contributor, Informed (DACI), as analytical lenses for accountability structure

**Out of scope:**
- Individual leadership capability failures unrelated to structural accountability design
- Failure modes in organisations with clear, non-overlapping accountability structures
- Detailed prescriptive redesign blueprints without empirical grounding

**Constraints:**
- Prefer primary empirical sources, including audit reports, inquiry reports, post-mortems, and empirical studies, over consulting opinion
- Every source must include a verifiable Uniform Resource Locator (URL)
- Acronyms expanded on first use throughout

## Context

- [inference; source: https://cisr.mit.edu/content/classic-topics-decision-rights; https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5413t3240/5413t3240.pdf; https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/corporate/reports/lessons-learned-transformation-pay-administration-initiative.html] This item treats accountability failure as an organisational-design problem because the recurring source pattern is mis-specified decision rights, role boundaries, and escalation authority rather than isolated individual mistakes.

## Approach

1. Define overlapping accountability and accountability gaps precisely, separating strategic-level and IT-delivery-level manifestations.
2. Gather empirical evidence from governance reviews, audit reports, inquiry reports, post-mortems, and empirical studies that attribute failure modes to these structural conditions.
3. Identify the most recurrent failure modes for strategic overlap, strategic absence, IT-layer overlap, IT-layer absence, and dual-layer combinations.
4. Compare single-layer and dual-layer cases to test whether severity rises when framing and execution are both weakly owned.
5. Review evidence on interventions, including clearer decision-right frameworks, ownership registries, and accountability models, and distinguish between well-evidenced remedies and weakly evidenced prescriptions.

### §2 Investigation

#### 2.1 Definition and baseline

- [fact; source: https://cisr.mit.edu/content/classic-topics-decision-rights] The MIT Center for Information Systems Research defines digital governance as a company's allocation of decision rights and accountabilities, and says that clear specification of both is key to designing digital organisations and making progress on digital transformation.
- [fact; source: https://scielo.org.za/scielo.php?script=sci_arttext&pid=S1560-683X2019000100011] Ako-Nai and Singh report that board-level IT governance depends on structural, process, and relational capabilities, and that oversight is weaker in outsourced IT arrangements.
- [inference; source: https://cisr.mit.edu/content/classic-topics-decision-rights; https://scielo.org.za/scielo.php?script=sci_arttext&pid=S1560-683X2019000100011] Overlapping accountability is best understood as duplicated decision authority without one decisive owner, while an accountability gap is missing authority or missing answerability for an outcome, because both conditions break the pairing between decision rights and consequences.

#### 2.2 Strategic-layer overlap and absence

- [fact; source: https://www.nao.org.uk/reports/the-national-programme-for-it-in-the-nhs-an-update-on-the-delivery-of-detailed-care-records-systems/; https://www.nao.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/1012888.pdf] The National Audit Office found that the National Programme for IT in the National Health Service was delivering far fewer systems than planned, that the original aim would not be achieved, and that scope had been reduced without a commensurate cost reduction.
- [fact; source: https://www.nao.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/1012888.pdf] The same report states that central programme contracts remained with the Department of Health while later support responsibility would transfer to National Health Service organisations that did not yet have direct contractual relationships with suppliers, leaving uncertainty over liabilities and future service transfer.
- [fact; source: https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/corporate/reports/lessons-learned-transformation-pay-administration-initiative.html] The Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat's lessons-learned report says government-wide transformation initiatives should assign accountability and authority to a single minister and deputy head, with other organisations' authorities, accountabilities, roles, and responsibilities documented in an overall accountability framework.
- [inference; source: https://www.nao.org.uk/reports/the-national-programme-for-it-in-the-nhs-an-update-on-the-delivery-of-detailed-care-records-systems/; https://www.nao.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/1012888.pdf; https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/corporate/reports/lessons-learned-transformation-pay-administration-initiative.html] Strategic-layer accountability overlap or absence produces slow or reversible decisions, weak benefit ownership, and unresolved liability questions because no single senior actor can settle trade-offs for the whole programme.

#### 2.3 IT-delivery-layer overlap and absence

- [fact; source: https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5413t3240/5413t3240.pdf] The Queensland Health Payroll System inquiry records differing expectations of roles and responsibilities between International Business Machines and client stakeholders as one reason tied to a formal delay notice.
- [fact; source: https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5413t3240/5413t3240.pdf] The inquiry also states as a project principle that successful delivery requires defined and agreed roles and responsibilities across business, user, and supplier interests, because projects fail when participants do not know what is expected of them or of others.
- [fact; source: https://files.gao.gov/reports/GAO-25-107795/index.html] The Government Accountability Office found that incomplete modernization plans for critical federal legacy systems increased the likelihood of cost overruns, schedule delays, and overall project failure.
- [fact; source: https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/oag-bvg/audit-reports/documents/ag-202603-modernizing-pay-system.pdf] The Auditor General of Canada reported that more than 233,000 pay transactions remained in backlog as of 30 September 2025, affecting more than 133,000 employees, and warned that unresolved backlog and slow simplification of pay rules risked carrying existing errors into the successor system.
- [inference; source: https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5413t3240/5413t3240.pdf; https://files.gao.gov/reports/GAO-25-107795/index.html; https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/oag-bvg/audit-reports/documents/ag-202603-modernizing-pay-system.pdf] At the IT-delivery layer, accountability overlap and absence appear operationally as unresolved open issues, change-control churn, weak risk ownership, and backlogs that no single team can burn down decisively.

#### 2.4 Outsourcing, shared services, and multi-party operating models

- [fact; source: https://scielo.org.za/scielo.php?script=sci_arttext&pid=S1560-683X2019000100011] Ako-Nai and Singh found that board IT oversight is weakest in outsourced IT arrangements.
- [fact; source: https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/oag-bvg/audit-reports/documents/ag-202603-modernizing-pay-system.pdf] The 2026 Canadian pay audit describes a process in which departments, agencies, the pay centre, and central organisations all hold distinct parts of the transaction path, making role clarity an explicit operating requirement rather than an optional governance overlay.
- [fact; source: https://www.nao.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/1012888.pdf] The National Audit Office recorded uncertainty about how financial liability and service-transfer mechanisms would work when programme support moved from the Department to local organisations.
- [inference; source: https://scielo.org.za/scielo.php?script=sci_arttext&pid=S1560-683X2019000100011; https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/oag-bvg/audit-reports/documents/ag-202603-modernizing-pay-system.pdf; https://www.nao.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/1012888.pdf] Shared-service and outsourced models magnify accountability failure because legal authority, delivery work, budget, and operational consequences sit in different places unless the hand-offs are deliberately designed.

#### 2.5 Dual-layer compounding effects

- [fact; source: https://www.nao.org.uk/reports/the-national-programme-for-it-in-the-nhs-an-update-on-the-delivery-of-detailed-care-records-systems/; https://www.nao.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/1012888.pdf] In the National Programme for IT in the National Health Service, scope reduction, delayed delivery, uncertain local responsibilities, and reduced realised benefits appeared together rather than as isolated problems.
- [fact; source: https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5413t3240/5413t3240.pdf] In the Queensland payroll case, governance review, project management failure, contractual friction, and delayed delivery were investigated as one coupled failure rather than as separate defects.
- [fact; source: https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/oag-bvg/audit-reports/documents/ag-202603-modernizing-pay-system.pdf; https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/corporate/reports/lessons-learned-transformation-pay-administration-initiative.html] In the Canadian pay transformation material, backlog persistence, incomplete simplification, and the recommendation for a single accountable minister and deputy head appear in the same reform narrative.
- [inference; source: https://www.nao.org.uk/reports/the-national-programme-for-it-in-the-nhs-an-update-on-the-delivery-of-detailed-care-records-systems/; https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5413t3240/5413t3240.pdf; https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/oag-bvg/audit-reports/documents/ag-202603-modernizing-pay-system.pdf; https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/corporate/reports/lessons-learned-transformation-pay-administration-initiative.html] Dual-layer failure is more damaging than single-layer failure because ambiguity exists both in who sets direction and in who owns execution, so the system cannot converge on a single corrective action when problems surface.

#### 2.6 Remedies and evidence quality

- [inference; source: https://cisr.mit.edu/content/classic-topics-decision-rights; https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/corporate/reports/lessons-learned-transformation-pay-administration-initiative.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-04-26-ai-lowcode-decision-rights-accountability-liability.html] The strongest repeated remedy across the evidence base is explicit allocation of decision rights and accountability to one clear owner for each consequential decision or outcome, plus documented cross-party roles and authorities.
- [fact; source: https://www.isaca.org/resources/cobit; https://teamtopologies.com/book] ISACA presents Control Objectives for Information and Related Technologies (COBIT) as a governance framework for enterprise information and technology, and Team Topologies presents clear team purpose and boundaries as central to sustained delivery, so both are useful design lenses for remediation.
- [inference; source: https://www.isaca.org/resources/cobit; https://teamtopologies.com/book; https://cisr.mit.edu/content/classic-topics-decision-rights] Control Objectives for Information and Related Technologies and Team Topologies are best treated as implementation lenses for naming roles, boundaries, and governance objectives once accountable owners have been defined for each consequential decision or service boundary.
- [assumption; source: https://www.hbs.edu/faculty/Pages/item.aspx?num=20375] Search note: direct extraction from the seeded Weill and Ross source was blocked in this session, so this item does not quote it directly.
- Search note: queries for direct primary studies evaluating RACI-specific intervention outcomes, including "RACI matrix empirical study role clarity project performance IT governance" and "RACI decision rights empirical study", did not surface an accessible primary study with extractable evidence in this session.

### §3 Reasoning

- [fact; source: https://cisr.mit.edu/content/classic-topics-decision-rights; https://scielo.org.za/scielo.php?script=sci_arttext&pid=S1560-683X2019000100011] The core factual thread is that governance quality depends on how decision rights and accountabilities are allocated and that oversight weakens in fragmented or outsourced arrangements.
- [fact; source: https://www.nao.org.uk/reports/the-national-programme-for-it-in-the-nhs-an-update-on-the-delivery-of-detailed-care-records-systems/; https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5413t3240/5413t3240.pdf; https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/oag-bvg/audit-reports/documents/ag-202603-modernizing-pay-system.pdf; https://files.gao.gov/reports/GAO-25-107795/index.html] The case evidence repeatedly shows delays, cost exposure, scope erosion, backlogs, and unresolved transition or liability questions.
- [inference; source: https://cisr.mit.edu/content/classic-topics-decision-rights; https://www.nao.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/1012888.pdf; https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5413t3240/5413t3240.pdf; https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/corporate/reports/lessons-learned-transformation-pay-administration-initiative.html] These recurring symptoms are best explained as accountability-structure failures when they align with documented uncertainty over authority, role boundaries, or final ownership.

### §4 Consistency Check

- [inference; source: https://cisr.mit.edu/content/classic-topics-decision-rights; https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/corporate/reports/lessons-learned-transformation-pay-administration-initiative.html] The retrieved governance sources align on the claim that clearer allocation of decision rights and accountability improves governance quality.
- [fact; source: https://www.nao.org.uk/reports/the-national-programme-for-it-in-the-nhs-an-update-on-the-delivery-of-detailed-care-records-systems/; https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5413t3240/5413t3240.pdf; https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/oag-bvg/audit-reports/documents/ag-202603-modernizing-pay-system.pdf] The case sources differ in domain and institutional setting, but they converge on the same observed symptom cluster: slow decisions, unresolved risks, unclear hand-offs, and lower realised value.
- [inference; source: https://www.isaca.org/resources/cobit; https://teamtopologies.com/book] Framework sources are consistent with the audit findings as design guidance, but they are weaker as proof of causal effectiveness than the audit and inquiry material.
- [inference; source: https://www.nao.org.uk/reports/the-national-programme-for-it-in-the-nhs-an-update-on-the-delivery-of-detailed-care-records-systems/; https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5413t3240/5413t3240.pdf; https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/oag-bvg/audit-reports/documents/ag-202603-modernizing-pay-system.pdf] The comparative severity claim remains medium confidence because the retrieved cases show a compounding pattern qualitatively, but they do not share a common quantitative benchmark.

### §5 Depth and Breadth Expansion

- [inference; source: https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5413t3240/5413t3240.pdf; https://www.nao.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/1012888.pdf] Behaviourally, overlapping accountability encourages deferral and defensive escalation because each actor can argue that another party owns the harder decision.
- [inference; source: https://www.nao.org.uk/reports/the-national-programme-for-it-in-the-nhs-an-update-on-the-delivery-of-detailed-care-records-systems/; https://files.gao.gov/reports/GAO-25-107795/index.html] Economically, accountability gaps convert governance ambiguity into direct waste by sustaining obsolete systems, prolonging expensive workarounds, and reducing benefits without proportionate cost reduction.
- [inference; source: https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/oag-bvg/audit-reports/documents/ag-202603-modernizing-pay-system.pdf; https://files.gao.gov/reports/GAO-25-107795/index.html] Technically, missing ownership turns simplification, backlog reduction, and system retirement into chronic queue-management problems rather than bounded engineering tasks.
- [fact; source: https://www.nao.org.uk/reports/the-national-programme-for-it-in-the-nhs-an-update-on-the-delivery-of-detailed-care-records-systems/; https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5413t3240/5413t3240.pdf; https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/corporate/reports/lessons-learned-transformation-pay-administration-initiative.html] Historically, the retrieved cases show that accountability defects survive restructures and remediation efforts unless reform explicitly names one accountable owner and rewrites cross-party responsibilities.

### §6 Synthesis

**Executive summary:**

Organisations with overlapping or absent accountability at strategic and information technology (IT) layers reliably show slower decisions, unresolved risk, and lower realised value than their programme plans imply. [inference; source: https://cisr.mit.edu/content/classic-topics-decision-rights; https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5413t3240/5413t3240.pdf; https://www.nao.org.uk/reports/the-national-programme-for-it-in-the-nhs-an-update-on-the-delivery-of-detailed-care-records-systems/; https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/oag-bvg/audit-reports/documents/ag-202603-modernizing-pay-system.pdf]

At the strategic layer, the recurring pattern is not merely poor leadership quality but weak ownership of decision rights, benefit accountability, liability, and escalation authority. [inference; source: https://cisr.mit.edu/content/classic-topics-decision-rights; https://www.nao.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/1012888.pdf; https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/corporate/reports/lessons-learned-transformation-pay-administration-initiative.html]

At the delivery layer, the observed symptoms are unresolved issues, change-control churn, backlog growth, and delayed modernization because no single actor can close open decisions across business, supplier, and operations boundaries. [inference; source: https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5413t3240/5413t3240.pdf; https://files.gao.gov/reports/GAO-25-107795/index.html; https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/oag-bvg/audit-reports/documents/ag-202603-modernizing-pay-system.pdf]

The strongest supported remedy is a clearly accountable owner for each consequential decision or outcome, with documented cross-party roles and decision rights, rather than undifferentiated shared ownership or a single blanket owner for every layer of the system. [inference; source: https://cisr.mit.edu/content/classic-topics-decision-rights; https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/corporate/reports/lessons-learned-transformation-pay-administration-initiative.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-04-26-ai-lowcode-decision-rights-accountability-liability.html]

**Key findings:**

1. [inference] **Where strategic accountability is overlapping or absent, organisations repeatedly lose decision speed and benefit ownership because no single senior actor can settle trade-offs across cost, scope, liability, and local implementation constraints.** (high confidence; source: https://cisr.mit.edu/content/classic-topics-decision-rights; https://www.nao.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/1012888.pdf; https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/corporate/reports/lessons-learned-transformation-pay-administration-initiative.html)
2. [inference] **At the information technology delivery layer, accountability failure shows up less as abstract governance weakness and more as concrete operational symptoms such as delay notices, unresolved open issues, backlog growth, and modernization work that cannot close cleanly.** (high confidence; source: https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5413t3240/5413t3240.pdf; https://files.gao.gov/reports/GAO-25-107795/index.html; https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/oag-bvg/audit-reports/documents/ag-202603-modernizing-pay-system.pdf)
3. [inference] **Shared-service and outsourced operating models are especially vulnerable because authority, contractual control, operational work, and consequences are split across organisations unless hand-offs and escalation rights are deliberately specified.** (high confidence; source: https://scielo.org.za/scielo.php?script=sci_arttext&pid=S1560-683X2019000100011; https://www.nao.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/1012888.pdf; https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/oag-bvg/audit-reports/documents/ag-202603-modernizing-pay-system.pdf)
4. [inference] **The most severe retrieved cases combine strategic-layer ambiguity with delivery-layer ambiguity alongside legacy-system complexity, contractual friction, or scope expansion, producing a coupled failure pattern in which programmes keep spending money while scope shrinks, liabilities stay unclear, and realised benefits lag far behind plan.** (medium confidence; source: https://www.nao.org.uk/reports/the-national-programme-for-it-in-the-nhs-an-update-on-the-delivery-of-detailed-care-records-systems/; https://www.nao.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/1012888.pdf; https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5413t3240/5413t3240.pdf; https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/oag-bvg/audit-reports/documents/ag-202603-modernizing-pay-system.pdf)
5. ### Executive Summary

Organisations with overlapping or absent accountability at strategic and information technology (IT) layers reliably show slower decisions, unresolved risk, and lower realised value than their programme plans imply. [inference; source: https://cisr.mit.edu/content/classic-topics-decision-rights; https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5413t3240/5413t3240.pdf; https://www.nao.org.uk/reports/the-national-programme-for-it-in-the-nhs-an-update-on-the-delivery-of-detailed-care-records-systems/; https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/oag-bvg/audit-reports/documents/ag-202603-modernizing-pay-system.pdf]

At the strategic layer, the recurring pattern is not merely poor leadership quality but weak ownership of decision rights, benefit accountability, liability, and escalation authority. [inference; source: https://cisr.mit.edu/content/classic-topics-decision-rights; https://www.nao.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/1012888.pdf; https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/corporate/reports/lessons-learned-transformation-pay-administration-initiative.html]

At the delivery layer, the observed symptoms are unresolved issues, change-control churn, backlog growth, and delayed modernization because no single actor can close open decisions across business, supplier, and operations boundaries. [inference; source: https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5413t3240/5413t3240.pdf; https://files.gao.gov/reports/GAO-25-107795/index.html; https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/oag-bvg/audit-reports/documents/ag-202603-modernizing-pay-system.pdf]

The strongest supported remedy is a clearly accountable owner for each consequential decision or outcome, with documented cross-party roles and decision rights, rather than undifferentiated shared ownership or a single blanket owner for every layer of the system. [inference; source: https://cisr.mit.edu/content/classic-topics-decision-rights; https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/corporate/reports/lessons-learned-transformation-pay-administration-initiative.html; https://davidamitchell.github.io/Research/research/2026-04-26-ai-lowcode-decision-rights-accountability-liability.html]

### Key Findings

1. **Where strategic accountability is overlapping or absent, organisations repeatedly lose decision speed and benefit ownership because no single senior actor can settle trade-offs across cost, scope, liability, and local implementation constraints.** ([inference]; high confidence; source: https://cisr.mit.edu/content/classic-topics-decision-rights; https://www.nao.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/1012888.pdf; https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/corporate/reports/lessons-learned-transformation-pay-administration-initiative.html)
2. **At the information technology delivery layer, accountability failure shows up less as abstract governance weakness and more as concrete operational symptoms such as delay notices, unresolved open issues, backlog growth, and modernization work that cannot close cleanly.** ([inference]; high confidence; source: https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5413t3240/5413t3240.pdf; https://files.gao.gov/reports/GAO-25-107795/index.html; https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/oag-bvg/audit-reports/documents/ag-202603-modernizing-pay-system.pdf)
3. **Shared-service and outsourced operating models are especially vulnerable because authority, contractual control, operational work, and consequences are split across organisations unless hand-offs and escalation rights are deliberately specified.** ([inference]; high confidence; source: https://scielo.org.za/scielo.php?script=sci_arttext&pid=S1560-683X2019000100011; https://www.nao.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/1012888.pdf; https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/oag-bvg/audit-reports/documents/ag-202603-modernizing-pay-system.pdf)
4. **The most severe retrieved cases combine strategic-layer ambiguity with delivery-layer ambiguity alongside legacy-system complexity, contractual friction, or scope expansion, producing a coupled failure pattern in which programmes keep spending money while scope shrinks, liabilities stay unclear, and realised benefits lag far behind plan.** ([inference]; medium confidence; source: https://www.nao.org.uk/reports/the-national-programme-for-it-in-the-nhs-an-update-on-the-delivery-of-detailed-care-records-systems/; https://www.nao.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/1012888.pdf; https://www.parliament.qld.gov.au/Work-of-the-Assembly/Tabled-Papers/docs/5413t3240/5413t3240.pdf; https://www.canada.ca/content/dam/oag-bvg/audit-reports/documents/ag-202603-modernizing-pay-system.pdf)
